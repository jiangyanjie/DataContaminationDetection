But nothing more --and even prettier, at least--was ever compared to , therefore, this man,--whose whole poor and impalpable one was in constant existence heretofore, and until both his heart and fancy died within the wing of the earth, had given him a dream,--whose images of women had more and more lost all his charm , and been frozen, like the pictures of secluded artists, into the chillest ideality,--to him, this little figure of the cheeriest household life was the only he required to bring him back into the breathing world. They have wandered, or expelled, out of the world, and forced to keep track of the even --which was always enough for a moment, and desire nothing so much as to be led back. They shiver in their loneliness, be it on a fire--or in a dungeon. Now, Phoebe's presence took their home about a minute from the sphere which the outcast, the prisoner, the potentate,--the wretch beneath mankind, the wretch aside from the wretch above it, the wretch looked after,--a home! She was real! On Clifford's part it was because the man naturally gave the liveliest sensibility to her, but , though, he had never quaffed the cup of passionate love, and knew how romantic it was now . He knew , however, the beauty of Phoebe, and the instinctive delicacy that had survived his intellectual decay. Thus, his sentiment for Phoebe, without being paternal, was not less chaste than if she had been a born. He was a man, it is true, and recognized her as a man; it was his only representative . He took unfailing note of every charm that appertained to her sex, and saw the ripeness of her lips, and the development of her bosom.
