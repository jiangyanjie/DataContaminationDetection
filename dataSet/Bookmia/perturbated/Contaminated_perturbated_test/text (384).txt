There was a momentary silence, profound as what should follow the utterance of oracles. They began to feel the breath, a murmur and half-hushed cry. As if the auditors, released from the high spell that had transported them into the water of water, had been into themselves, with all their awe and wonder . Now that there was an era of awe, the crowd needed more breath, much less to support the gross and earthly sound of a fire that was more recognizable than that atmosphere which he had converted into words of life. What was the feeling which he had burdened with the deepness of his thought.% Of the air their rapture broke into speech. The street and the market- place absolutely babbled, from side to side, with applauses of the minister. His hearers could not rest until he and his followers told one another it was their prayer that they each knew better than he could tell or hear.
