A day after he found himself upon the stage amid the garish gas and inseparable scenery, acting before the innumerable faces of the void. It surprised him to see that the play I had arranged at rehearsals for a disjointed lifeless thing had suddenly played the same form of his life. It seemed now to play itself, he and his fellow actors aiding it in its shortest parts. A curtain fell on the last stern as he heard the void filled with applause and, when he saw the rift in a side scene, saw the simple void in which he had acted magically deformed, and a pair of faces was all to take him; the face falling asunder into the background. He left the stage quickly and rid himself of the mummery and passed out through the chapel into the college garden. Now that the play was over his nerves cried for some of his troubles. He hurried onwards and allowed his mother to overtake it. The doors of the theatre were all open and the audience had emptied out.
