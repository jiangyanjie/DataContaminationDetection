Well, I went fooling the deep woods till I judged . And from the foot of the wood, I had my gun on the roof, and hadn't shot nothing; it was for protection; thought I would kill some game nigh home. About this time I mighty near stepped on a stump, and it went sliding off through the grass, flowers, and I after it, trying to get a shot at it. I clipped along, and all of a sudden I bounded on my hands to the sound of a camp fire that was still smoking. My heart jumped up amongst my lungs. I never waited for to look further, but uncocked my gun and went sneaking back on my own, and to me, as fast as ever I could. Then I was up in the woods and was hungry for about two hours; but I didn't see nothing, I didn't hear nothing ; had I heard and seen as much as a thousand things.
