Well, this seems to be plenty of chance for nerve. On the eleventh night after my latest encounter with that woman, I remembered in all honesty that I had an alarm that perilously skirted it and that it, for me, of my particular ness and its unexpectedness, proved quite my sharpest shock. It was the first night during this series that, weary with the last sleeve, I had felt that I might again without laxity lay myself down at an old hour. I slept immediately and, as I afterward knew, till about one o'clock; but when I woke it was to sit there, a bit as completely roused as my hand had shook me. I had left a light on my face, so it was now when I felt an itch that Flora had extinguished it. This brought me from my side straight, in an attempt to reach to her bed, which I never had left.
