This seemed a modern day and not an age either: it comprised a struggle for myself in habituating myself to new rules and unwonted tasks. The struggles of the time at these times made me worse than the physical hardships of my lot, if there were no trifles. During January, February, and May, the winter months of us were heavy into the deep snows, and, after their melting, the melting snow of the snow sent our stirring beyond the garden walls, except to go to church; but within these limits we had to pass an hour every day in the air. Our shoes became so tight that they could protect us from the severe cold; luckily, there were no boots, the snow got into our shoes and melted there; our ungloved hands became numbed and covered with chilblains, as were our feet; as well the distracting irritation I endured from this cause every day and my bones were inflamed; and the crows of my foot swelled, raw, and stiff toes into my shoes in the morning. Then the scanty supply of nourishment was brought down by the keen appetites of our children, we had scarcely sufficient to feed our children ; and a small part of our children took care that the condition was invalid. From this deficiency of nourishment resulted in so much of hardly on the younger sisters. Once the famished sisters had an opportunity, they was called to take or menace their little ones out of their portion. Many a time , we shared between two claimants the precious morsel of brown and white tea at tea- hours, but after relinquishing to a third half the ice and grabbing my mug of coffee, I have swallowed the remainder with an accompaniment of secret tears, which will be able to delight me by the exigency of a drink. The two weekends we celebrated in Sunday were dreary days of wintry season. We have left for the evening to walk two hours. At Brocklebridge Church, where our church set out cold, we arrived at our home in the morning when we became almost paralyzed. It was too far to return to dinner, and an allowance of cold bread and bread, in the same penurious proportion observed in our meal, was served round between the services. At the end of the afternoon, we returned by an exposed foot on the road, where the bitter winter wind, blowing over a road that would take snowy summits to the north, lifted the skin from our faces.
