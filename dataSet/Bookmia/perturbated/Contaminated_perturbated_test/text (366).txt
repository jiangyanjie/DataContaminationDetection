Before a new day, in my room, had fully broken, my eyes opened to Mrs. Grose, and I had come to my bedside with worse news. Flora was so feverish that an illness was perhaps at the back of her head. My friend, who had a long night of extreme unrest, was just so agitated above all the people that had for their subject not in mind her former, but wholly her present, governess. Immediately she was very confused with the possible re-entrance of Miss Jessel on the scene that she protested-- I felt so intensely passionately against what her friend would take on her, of course, and with no deal to be done, I had seen the truth in her mind that my friend had discernibly now girded her loins to me. . This I felt as soon as I was young, and put to her the question of her sense of the child's sincerity as against my own. "She persists in denying to you that she 's the most difficult man her life has ever seen, and that the visitor's trouble, truly, was great. "Ah, miss, it isn't a thing in which I can push her!
