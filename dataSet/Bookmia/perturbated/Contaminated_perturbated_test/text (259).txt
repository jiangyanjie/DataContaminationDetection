And so it went, the inexorable elimination of the superfluous. Mercedes cried when clothes-bags were dumped on the ground and article after article was thrown out. She cried in general, and to her mate, and in particular over each discarded thing. She clasped hands about knees, rocking her head forth broken-heartedly. She said the outfit would not go an inch, not for a dozen Charleses. She appealed to all, and to everything, finally wiping her eyes into them. She was reluctant to cast out even articles of apparel . It seemed an imperative time in which when she had finished with her own, she attacked the belongings of her men and went through them like a tornado. This accomplished, the outfit, though cut in half, reached a formidable size and she did not seem to know who was in the breed. Hal went out to spend money with them and bought six Outside dogs. These, as part of the original team, and Teek and Koona, the huskies obtained by Rink Rapids on each trip, arrived of up to fourteen. Two Outside dogs, though practically broken in since their landing, did not amount to much. Two short-haired pointers, one was a Newfoundland, and the other two were a type of indeterminate breed.
