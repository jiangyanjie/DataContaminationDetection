In the end he , at that time lost the benefit of his early education. While his work, begun soon and concluded late, had extinguished any curiosity he had in his work, he showed no sign of the role he played in his books or learn- ing. His childhood's sense of superiority instilled into him by the favours of Mr. Paul faded away. He struggled long to keep up an equality with Catherine in her studies, and yielded a though silent regret; but he yielded completely, and there was no prevailing on him to take a step in the way of learning.
