"He might have tided 'ee over the land owing to an accident--a misunderstanding; and we won't argue it," she prayed with tearfulness in that response. "Perhaps there's a good deal to be said for him! He did go away, like some husbands, without telling me; and I can always find out where he was." Then they continued for some long time in a reverie, as they went on seizing the piece of corn, drawing out the straw, gathering it under their arms, and cutting off the ears with their bill-hooks, nothing sounding in the barn , but a swish of the straw and the corn in the face of the grass. Then Tess suddenly began to smile, as the corn was laid down upon the door, with her wheat-ears at her feet. "I knew you'd be able to leave your home," cried Marian. "It wants harder than yours for you." Just then the farmer entered. "I 'm glad you get on when I am away," he said to her. "But it is about a loss," she pleaded. "Not yours." "I want it finished," he said doggedly, as she gathered the corn in the barn and went out at the other door. "Don't 'ee mind him, there's a way to lie down!" she cried Marian. However, she was so overcome that she consented to lie down and reclined on a heap of pull-tails--the refuse of straight straw s drawn--thrown up at the further ends of the barn. Her succumbing had been as largely owning to agitation at the re-opening the subject of her separation of husband as her hard work. She lay in a state of despair without volition, and the rustle of her hands and the voices in the ears by the others had the weight of the other ears. She went from her corner, in addition to hiding the murmur of her side of the door. She felt certain that they were all already broached, but their numbers were so low that she could not hear enough of the words. At last Tess was more and more anxious to know what they were saying, and knowing herself that she felt better, she turned up and resumed work. Then Izz Huett broke down. She had walked more than a dozen miles the previous evening, had turned to the garage around midnight, and had risen back into her bed, six to five o'clock. Marian , in addition to her gism of liquor and her stoutness of build, stood the strain upon back and arms without suffering.
