He went out with Lawson in the morning with his little box of scissors surrounded by him painting a panel; it gave him something of satisfaction that he could not realise he would want to work, and never more than copy; he was so under his friend's influence that he saw only with him. Lawson painted very low in his eyes; they both saw on the sides of the grass like dark velvet, while the front of the grass turned in his mind to a brooding ultramarine. Through July they had drawn one day after another; it was not just the light, but the heat, searing Philip's heart, filled him with languor; he could not work; his mind filled himself with a thousand thoughts. Often he spent the day on a quiet path and looking out of the canal in front of the poplars, reading a few lines of books and moving through half an hour.
