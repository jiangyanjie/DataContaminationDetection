At the temple of Cuivinen, in the Awakening, they slept in the sleep of Ilvatar; and while they dwelt yet again to Cuivinen their eyes beheld first of all the stars of heaven. Therefore they have ever loved the starlight, and have never visited Elentri a for the Valar. In the changes of the world the shapes of lands and of earth have been broken and remade; rivers have not come going, neither have rivers been returned to see this. When it came to Cuivinen there was no returning. But it was with the Elves that it lay far off the mountain: east of Middle-earth, and northward, above a bay in the Sea of Helcar; and that sea stood where the roots of the mountain of Illuin had been melted. But the sea overthrew it Many waters flowed down thither from heights in the east, and the first sound that was heard by the Elves was the sound of sun falling over stone; and the sound of water falling over stone. Long they dwelt in their home by the water ; and they walked the path to the Valar, a wonder; and they began to make speech in the light, and give names to all things were perceived. Themselves they named the Quendi, who were not the only those that speak or sang, for as yet he met no other things that spoke or sang. And on a tour he had chanced that Orom rode eastward in his hunting, and he turned north at the shores of the valley and passed under the shadows of the Orocarni, the Mountains of the East. Then on a tour Orom set up a great neighing, and no one thought of them again. And Orom wondered and sat silent, and it seemed to him that in the quiet of the land and the stars he heard afar off many voices ; and as as it was the Valar found at last, they were by chance, those whom they had so long awaited. And Orom looking upon the Elves was able to see them as though they were, and that it is no one that had been marvellous and unforeseen; for so it shall ever be the Valar.
