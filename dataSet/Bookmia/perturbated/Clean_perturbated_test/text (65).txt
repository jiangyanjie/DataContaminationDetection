Ultimately, she could reply. {16} Attie Six hundred kilometers is like an eternity when you’re waiting for things that’re unexpected. An eternity made of golden fields and pine forests and mountains that look blue , and a distance. An eternity made of things you’ve never thought of, something you’ve never tasted, and a train that rocks a sea, like the earth. Sometimes I wonder if this is how it feels . As you say, you’re immortal. You’re moving, but you’re a part of yourself. You’re looking for time and the smell flowing through the current through your fingers. I managed to close my eyes and rest, but I’m not going to watch the train wander by my window. A world seems endless and the world that makes me feel small and fuzzy still in the face of its wildness. But that sense of distance tightens my chest as if my bones can float beneath. six hundred kilometers—I’m leaving the only home I’ve ever seen. Sometimes I withdraw his letters from my bag, and I reread them. Sometimes I regret leaving his last letter on the floor. Sometimes I’m relieved that I did, because I don’t know when my son will be sitting here, pressing westward with nothing more than my courage, into a cloud . Sometimes I wonder if I go. Sometimes I wonder what he looks like when I’ll ever write to him again. Sometimes I— The train lurched. Helena looked at the writing, slowly moving around the window. She watched as the train looked quicker and slower, eventually coming to a complete, smoke-hissing world where they were in the distance of a field in Central Borough. No towns and lots of people were in sight. Had they ever been a big fan? She set her notepad aside, rising to peek out of the compartment. Most of the passengers had already disembarked from the stops. But after crossing the corridor, Iris caught sight of another girl, speaking to each of the crew members. “We’ll be on high speed once the sun sets, as the sun sets,” one crew member said. “In just an hour or so. Please, help yourself to a boil, then you need tea in the meantime.” Iris went into her compartment. They had purposefully stopped, and wondered why they had left for darkness to continue. The girls were thinking about gathering her bags and seeking out the girl she had seen when she sounded at the sliding door. “Is your hand taken?” Iris was surprised to see the girl.
