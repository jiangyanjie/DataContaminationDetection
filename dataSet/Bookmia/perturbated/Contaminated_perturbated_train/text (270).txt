Faber backed up, as if he feared the book would vanish if Faber had looked at the book and pulled his eyes from it. Behind him, Montag's door to a bedroom stood open, and a case litter with iron, wood and steel tools was strewn across at the desk-top. Montag had only a glimpse, before Faber, seeing Montag's attention diverted, turned quickly and slowly upon the bedroom door and stood holding the door with a trembling hand. Montag returned unsteadily to the room where he was now seated with the book in his lap. "The book-where I go," he said. "I love it."
