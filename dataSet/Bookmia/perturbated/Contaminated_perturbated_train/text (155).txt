The story really began in the middle sixties, the period of the great war in which the original leaders of the Revolution were wiped out once and for all. By 1970 none of the survivors were terribly successful, except Big Brother , but the rest had by that time been exposed as traitors and counter-revolutionaries. So some fled and were murdered on one -off and in 1975 one had disappeared. For others, a few had simply disappeared, while the majority had been executed in trials at which they made confession of their crimes. Among the last survivors were three men named Jones, Aaronson, and Rutherford. It could have been in 1965 that these three had been arrested. After nothing happened, they had vanished for three years or more revealing that one did not see how they were alive or dead, and then had suddenly been brought forth to incriminate his people in the usual way. They had confessed to intelligence with regard to them (at that date, too, the enemy was Eurasia), embezzlement of public funds, abuse of Communist Party members, rebellion under the leadership of Big Brother which had killed hundreds of people before the Revolution happened, and acts of sabotage causing the death of hundreds and thousands of people. After confessing to an earlier prison sentence in 1954, they had been reinstated in the UK and given posts which in fact sinecures that were not the biggest subjects in a century. All three had written long, abject articles in the UK before discovering the reasons for their defection and promising to live on. Some time after their release Winston had found all three of them in the Chestnut Tree Cafe. Winston had begun to experience the twisted and terrified fascination with which he had watched him from the bottom of the corner of the road. They had so far been those who had a fascination with their predecessors and had been in the relics of the ancient world, almost the last great figures left over in the heroic days of the Party.
