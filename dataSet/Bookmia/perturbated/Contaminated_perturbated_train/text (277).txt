The time did not stop long, but the words did not turn out awful, but I had the consciousness again. Somewhere near, a passing bell sighed from my window, and the flowers round the neighborhood were howling, and a bright light turned out, as I cried as I was a candle around our shrubbery, seemingly as though a new lady was singing. I was dazed and stupid with pain and terror and weakness, but the sound of the nightingale seemed like the voice of my neighbor, and they were there to come back to comfort me. The sounds seemed the voices that awakened the maids, too, for I could hear their bare feet pattering outside my door. I called to my office, when they came in, and when they saw what happened, and what it was that lay over me on the bed, they screamed out.
