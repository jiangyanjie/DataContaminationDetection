We judged it expedient, but I said by her all we knew; which I recounted at the last minute. She listened with great attention, with a face that often changed, but had the same purpose in all its depths. Her eyes were adorned with tears, but those she witnessed seemed as if the spirit were quite altered, and she could not be said. She asked, when all is changed where she had to be communicated with, if occasion should arise. Under the lamp in the road, I put my two addresses on a book, in my pocket-book, which I tore out and gave to her, and which she put in her purse; when I asked her where she was.
