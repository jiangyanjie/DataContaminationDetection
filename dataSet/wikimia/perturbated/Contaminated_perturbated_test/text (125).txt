The negotiations between Israel and the Palestinians began on April 21, 2014, following an attempt by United States Secretary of State John Kerry to restart the peace process. Martin Indyk, a senior fellow at the Brookings Institution in Washington, D.C. , was named as the person by the US to oversee the negotiations. Indyk had served as U.S. ambassador to Israel and assistant secretary of state for Near East and African affairs during the Clinton administration. Hamas, the Islamist faction that controls Gaza, rejected Kerry's announcement, stating that president Mahmoud Abbas has no authority to negotiate in the name of the Palestinian people.The negotiations are expected to last up to nine months to reach a final status to the Palestinian-Israeli conflict by mid-2014.
