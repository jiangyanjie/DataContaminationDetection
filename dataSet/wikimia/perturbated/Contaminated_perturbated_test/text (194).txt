Protests against the 2013 Egyptian coup d'état erupted in July 2013. Immediately following the removal of President Mohamed Morsi by the Egyptian army from power (4 July – 3 August) amid demonstrations against Morsi's rule, many protesters amassed near the Rabia square to call for Morsi's return to power and condemn the military, while others dispersed from the square in support of the protests against Morsi 's government. Deadly violence known as the Rabia massacre continued for several days, with three particularly bloody incidents described by officials as being perpetrated by Morsi supporters. During the holy month of Ramadan (10 July – 7 August), prime minister Hazem al-Beblawy threatened to disperse the ongoing Pro-Morsi sit-ins in Rabaa al-Adaweya square and al-Nahda square.
