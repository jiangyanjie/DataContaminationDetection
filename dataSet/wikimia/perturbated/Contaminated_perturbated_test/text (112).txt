The Abkhazian Revolution took place in 2014, when President Aleksandr Ankvab resigned after mass demonstrators stormed his office. Following mass protests in the capital Sukhumi and occupation of his office on 28 May, Ankvab fled to his hometown of Gudauta and ultimately resigned on 1 June, after treating the demonstration as an act d'Ã©tat.The uprising was attributed to public anger with Ankvab over his foreign policy toward ethnic Georgians in Abkhazia, a republic with limited recognition. Although receiving diplomatic recognition from Georgia in 2012, the Ankvab administration allowed ethnic Georgians only to register as voters and receive visas.The revolution led to an early presidential election being called in August 2014.
