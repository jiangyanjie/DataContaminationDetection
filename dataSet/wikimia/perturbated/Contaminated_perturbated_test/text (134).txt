The Toronto Raptors are a lacrosse team based in Toronto playing in the National Lacrosse League (NLL). The 2014 season was the 17th in the franchise's history, now known as the Rock. After an off-season injury-plagued start to the season, the Raptors acquired veteran goaltender Brandon Miller from the Philadelphia Wings at the trade deadline to revitalize their shaky defense. On April 5, star forward Garrett Billings tore his ACL during a game against the Vancouver Stealth and was lost for the rest of the season. Despite being the league's scoring leader, the Raptors won that game, which started a four-game winning streak to finish the regular season with 14 wins and clinch second place in the Eastern division ahead of the slumping Nashville Bandits.
