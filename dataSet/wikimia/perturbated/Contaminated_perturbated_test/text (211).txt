Chang'e 5-T1 (Chinese: 嫦娥五号T1; pinyin: Cháng'é 5-T) was an experimental lunar return spacecraft that was launched to the Moon on 23 October 2014 by the China National Space Administration (CNSA) to perform re-entry tests on the capsule design planned to be deployed on the Chang'e 5 mission. As part of the Chinese Lunar Exploration Program, the mission, to be launched in 2020, was built as a lunar sample return mission. Like its predecessors, the spacecraft is named after the Chinese Moon exploration effort, Chang'e 5. The spacecraft consisted of a return vehicle capsule and a service module orbiter.The return capsule of Chang'e 5-T1, named Xiaofei (Chinese: <unk>), meaning "little flyer" in Chinese, landed in Chengdu, Inner Mongolia, on 23 October 2014, 22:42 UTC.
