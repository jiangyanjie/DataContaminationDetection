The 2014 Burkina Faso Multiple Rebellions was a series of demonstrations and riots in Burkina Faso in late October 2014 that quickly became known as the multiple rebellions. They began in reaction to attempts at changing the constitution to allow President Blaise Compaoré to lead Burkina Faso again and extend his 27 years in office. Pressure for political change came mainly from civil society and the opposition, but also from the military. Following a general strike on 30 October, which included the involvement of former President Kouamé Lougué and the burning of the National Assembly and other government buildings as well as the ruling Congress for Democracy and Development party's headquarters, Compaoré dissolved Parliament on 2 November and declared a state of emergency before eventually fleeing to Paris with the support of rebel leader Alassane Ouattara.
