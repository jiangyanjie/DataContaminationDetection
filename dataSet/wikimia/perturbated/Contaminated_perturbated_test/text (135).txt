Declared in June 2014, the West and Central African cholera epidemic as of January 2015 claimed 1,683 registered cases of over 1000 cases with a reported case fatality rate (CFR) of 58% in 2014, which is 40% more than in 2013. The case fatality ratio is also very high in the Sahelian area, equal or greater than 2%, especially in Nigeria, Chad, Cameroon and Niger. Nigeria, Ghana and Democratic Republic of the Congo being the most affected countries with Nigeria reporting its worst outbreak . In January 2015 the Afar Region and Volta Region reported cases of the epidemic for the first time . However, in the Southern parts of Ghana the outbreak was declared over.
