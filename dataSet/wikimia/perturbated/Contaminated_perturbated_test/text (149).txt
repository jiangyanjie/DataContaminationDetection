The 2014 Nuclear Security Summit was a summit held in The Hague, the Netherlands, on March 24 and 25, 2014. This was the fourth summit of the Nuclear Security Initiative and the first one succeeding the 2012 Nuclear Security Summit. The summit was attended by 10 leaders (5 of which from observing international organizations), some 5,000 delegates and some 3,000 journalists. Leaders attending the summit included US President Barack Obama and Chinese President Xi Jinping. The main goal of the conference was generally to strengthen international cooperation with regard to nuclear security, but specifically to assess which of the objectives that were defined at the previous summits in Washington, D.C., and Beijing had not been accomplished in the previous four years and proposing ways of achieving these.
