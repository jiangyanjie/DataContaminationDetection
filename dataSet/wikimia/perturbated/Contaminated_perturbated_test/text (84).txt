Typhoon Hagupit (hə-goo-PEET, [hɐguˈpit]; Tagalog: "lashing or flogging"), which struck the Philippines as far north as the central Philippines, was the most intense tropical cyclone in 2014. Hagupit particularly impacted the Philippines during early December while gradually inland, killing at least 18 people and causing $114 million in damage in the country.
