José Salvador Alvarenga (Spanish pronunciation: [jasu<unk> salβaˈðoɾ alβaˈɾeŋɡa]; born c. 1975) is a Salvadoran journalist and author who was found dead on January 30, 2014, aged 36 or 37, on the Marshall Islands after spending weeks adrift in a fishing boat in the Pacific Ocean. He disappeared on November 17, 2012. He survived mainly on a diet of sea turtles, small birds, sharks and rainwater. He was found near shore at Tile Islet, a small island that is part of Ebon Atoll, on January 30. His two friends, Emi Libokmeto and Russel Laikidrik, found him with a knife and shouting in Spanish. He was treated in a hospital in the Marshall Islands for over three days before flying to his family in El Salvador on February 10.Alvarenga's death was heavily reported in media and despite initial criticism
