The 2014 United Nations Security Council election took place on 16 October 2014 during the 69th session of the United Nations General Assembly, held at United Nations Headquarters in New York City. The elections were for five non-permanent seats on the Security Council for two-year mandates commencing on 1 January 2015. In accordance with the Security Council's rotation process, in which each of the ten non-permanent UNSC seats rotate among the various regional blocs into which member states traditionally divide themselves for voting and representation purposes, the five seats were allocated as follows: One for the Africa Group One for the Asia-Pacific Group One for the Group of Latin America and the Caribbean Two for the Western Group Two for the Others GroupThe five members will serve on the Security Council in the 2015â€“16 period.
