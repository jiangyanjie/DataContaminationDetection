General elections were held in Fiji on 17 September 2014 to select the 50 seats in Parliament. The United For Fiji party led by Prime Minister Frank Bainimarama won a landslide victory, winning 32 of the 50 seats. The Social Democratic Party and the National Party were the only other two parties to reach the 5% electoral threshold and win seats. Elections had originally been scheduled for March 2009, but were postponed because politicians did not agree with the Prime Minister's Platform for Change, Peace and Progress. Between 2009 and 2013 several petitions and requests were made for elections, and on 23 September 2014 the Fijian government announced that elections would be held on 17 September 2014.
