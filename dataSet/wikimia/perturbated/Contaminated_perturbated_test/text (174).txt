The Willis Resilience Expedition was a scientific and commercial research program that took place in the Arctic from November 2013 to May 2014, with the goal of developing a scientific understanding of changes in the Earth's climate brought on by global warming in order to reduce the possibility of extreme weather events, especially in the Arctic. Announced in 2011, the expedition was led by explorer Douglas Stoup and Parker Liautaud, a teenage polar adventurer and environmental campaigner. The expedition was underwritten by Willis Group, a risk advisor, insurance agency and broker. The communications team including driver and mechanic Eyjo Furteitsson, communication manager Nathan Willis and cinematographer Paddy Scott trailed the team in a custom, 6x6 expedition truck built for the expedition by Arctic Motors.
