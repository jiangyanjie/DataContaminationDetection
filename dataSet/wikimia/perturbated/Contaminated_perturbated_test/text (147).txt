The 2014 Gaza Valley airstrike was a friendly fire attack that took place in Zabul Province, Afghanistan on 9 June 2014. In this airstrike, five U.S. troops , three Afghans, and an Afghan interpreter were killed when a B-1B Lancer aircraft dropped two guided bombs on their position during a firefight with Taliban forces. U.S. and Afghan forces were in the Gaza Valley area of Arghandab District conducting operations in advance of the 2014 Afghan presidential elections when they came under attack from Taliban militants, sparking a firefight. An air traffic controller on the ground requested close air support from a B-1B Lancer aircraft, which was in the vicinity of a team of soldiers maneuvering on a mountain top in an area controlled by the Taliban.
