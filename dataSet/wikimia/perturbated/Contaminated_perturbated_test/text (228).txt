2014–2016 Croatian war veterans was 555 days long war veterans protest, often dubbed by the public as the "Certificate of the Last Month" (Croatian: Sra<unk>ovi<unk>). While the protests were initially aimed against Ministry of Croatian Veterans Predrag Glavašević and Minister Predrag Matić , the protesters subsequently changed their demands and started demanding the resignation of left-wing Prime Minister Zoran Milanović and then President Ivo Josipović. The protests were officially ended by the police in May 2017, when a group of protesters breached police cordons in Zagreb Upper Town and barricaded themselves around St Mark Church, where they clashed with the police.
