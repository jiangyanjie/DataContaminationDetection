Arturo (1985 – July 3, 2016) was a polar bear living in the National Park in Mendoza, Argentina, the only polar bear living in that country. He was born in the United States and transferred to Argentina in 1993. His mother, a female named Pelusa, died of cancer in 2012. The living conditions of polar bear Arturo are controversial, as temperatures reach up to 40 °C (121 °F) in Argentina, and the pool in which he lives was only 51 cm (18 inches) deep. Animal rights activists had, in 2010 called Arturo the "world's bear" and promoted a petition to have him moved to Assiniboine Park Zoo, a zoo in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada.
