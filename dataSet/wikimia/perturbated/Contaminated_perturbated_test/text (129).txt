The following addresses a recent battle in Northern Afghanistan on April 22, 2014. While this part of the country had long been relatively peaceful compared to the all-out conflict that existed in the rest of Afghanistan, the increasing insurgency and militant militancy would flare up again in February 2014 when German troops that had been deployed to the northern region came under attack more often, leading to the deaths of the several soldiers. Previously hindered by national and deteroriating security measures allowed the German-led Regional Command North and Central Central to launch a series of operations to take on the rising insurgency. Concerted operations began after an insurgent attack on Berlin and Kunduz within minutes of German Chancellor Angela Merkel's departure from a visit. During the next two years, the German presence would be doubled with reinforcements from an ISAF contingent
