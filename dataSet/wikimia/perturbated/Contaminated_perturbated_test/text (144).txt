The concept of Targeted Poverty Alleviation (Chinese: 精准扶贫; traditional Chinese: jīng h<unk>i pín) was first raised by Xi Jinping, General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party to combat poverty in China. Targeted poverty alleviation plays a key role in China's poverty alleviation strategy. Its purpose is to advance the Party's century goal of “comprehensively building a “moderately prosperous society” that is the first part of Xi Jinping’s agenda of Third Five Comprehensives. In his speeches, Xi Jinping repeatedly emphasizes that without addressing the poverty problem in rural areas, China will never become a moderately prosperous society.The Targeted Poverty Alleviation Strategy was officially adopted by the Chinese government in 2014.
