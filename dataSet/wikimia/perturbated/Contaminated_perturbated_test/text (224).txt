A by-election was held in the Cook Islands constituency of Mitiaro on 11 November 2014.In the July 2014 general election, balloting for the Mitiaro seat resulted in a tie. Incumbent candidate Joe Vavia, of the Democratic Party, received 50 votes, but so did Cook Islands Party candidate James Smith. The tie was confirmed by a recount, prompting the High Court to call a by-electionThe same two candidates participated in the by-election. The by-election took place on 4 December, but ballot counting was limited due to a legal challenge by the Cook Islands Party. The court ultimately ruled that a person had voted illegally in the Mitiaro constituency in the general election, and invalidated that person's ballot.
