Spy Booth was an artwork by Banksy in Cheltenham, England. The piece has been seen as a symbol of the artist's actions during the social media disclosures of 2013.In 2014, Robin Barton of the Art London helped with the removal of the artwork, and attempted to broker the removal and sale of the piece. However the artwork was painted onto a Grade II listed building (22 King Street) and the council ruled it from being removed, only giving it retrospective listed building consent in 2015 and affording it some time for removal. Despite this, the artwork was removed, and sold in August 2016. Spy Booth was auctioned as nine stucco-faced brick fragments sawed from the wall and as one piece.
