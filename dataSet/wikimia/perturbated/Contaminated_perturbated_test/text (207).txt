General elections were held in Costa Rica on Sunday, 2 February 2014 to elect a new president, nine cabinet presidents, and 57 Legislative Assembly lawmakers. In accordance with Article 132 of the constitution, incumbent President Laura Chinchilla Miranda was not allowed to run for a second consecutive term.The leftist Democratic Liberation Party of Costa Rica put forward San José Mayor Gabriel Monge as its presidential candidate; the Libertarian Movement nominated former legislator Otto Guevara Guth; the leftist Broad Front nominated José María Villalta; and the Citizens' Action Party nominated Luis Guillermo Solís .
