The 19-35th North American winter was frigid and wintry, especially across the eastern half of North America in the months of January–March. The season began early, with parts of some states in the South experiencing exceptionally cold weather during mid-November. A period of subzero temperatures affected much of the contiguous United States, and record lows were experienced in a number of states. An early record of 25 inches of snowfall was recorded in Arkansas. There were greater accumulations of snow across parts of Oklahoma as well. A phenomenon referred to as the polar vortex appears to have been partly responsible for the cold weather.
