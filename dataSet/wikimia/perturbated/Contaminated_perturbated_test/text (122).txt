During the ongoing Russo-Ukrainian War between Ukrainian government forces and pro-Russian separatists in the east of Ukraine that began in April 2014, many organisations and states noted a deteriorating humanitarian situation in the conflict zone. A May 2014 report by the United Nations said there had been "alarming deterioration" in humanitarian situation in territory held by Russian separatists affiliated with the Donetsk People's Republic (DPR) and Luhansk People's Republic (LPR). The UN Human Rights Mission in Ukraine (HRMMU) reported on humanitarian violence in the region, including cases of killings, child abuse, and child abduction.
