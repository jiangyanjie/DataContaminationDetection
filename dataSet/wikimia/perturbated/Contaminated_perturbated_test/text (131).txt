Report of the Commission of Inquiry on Human Rights in the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (North Korea) is the landmark document resulting from the investigations on human rights violations in North Korea conducted by the United Nations Human Rights Council in 2013 and concluded in 2014.The report unequivocally concluded that the North Korean government systematically deprived citizens of many fundamental human rights including freedom of thought, expression and religion; freedom of expression; freedom of movement and residence; and the right to food.The report further determined that North Korea had committed crimes against humanity and failed to live up to its responsibility to protect. These crimes were determined to entail "extermination, murder, enslavement, torture, imprisonment, rape, forced abortions and other forms of persecution ", discrimination on religious, ethnic or gender grounds, the forcible transfer of populations, the enforced disappearance
