On the morning of 18 November 2014, two Palestinian men from the Kehilat Bnei Torah prayer hall arrived in the Meir Nof neighborhood of West Jerusalem and attacked the praying crowd with axes, knives, and a gun. They killed four dual-nationality worshippers, and injured a responding Druze and a young man. One of the attackers later died of his injuries and was never found. The two also injured two male worshippers, one of whom woke up from a coma and died two days later. The two men were then shot dead by the Israelis. Many initial reports claimed that the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine (PFLP) had been responsible for the attack; however, Israeli authorities say declarations by PFLP militants were either confused or that the group itself disclaimed responsibility.
