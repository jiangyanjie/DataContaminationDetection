The child sexual abuse scandal consists of the allegations of child sexual abuse that occurred in the town of Rotherham, South Yorkshire, England from the late 1980s until the 2010s and the failure of local authorities to act on reports of the abuse for most of that period. Researcher Angie Davies, who was hired by local officials and was told about child exploitation occurring between 1994 and 2007, has since described the scandal as "the worst child sexual abuse scandal in the county's history". Evidence of the abuse was first noted in the late 1990s, when care home staff reported reports that children in their care were being picked up by taxi drivers.
