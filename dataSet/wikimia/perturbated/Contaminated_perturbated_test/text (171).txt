The GSK China scandal was an imbroglio where the China branch of the global drugmaker GlaxoSmithKline (GSK) pleaded guilty to paying bribes to doctors and medical experts in order to promote the company in China. The company's investigation in Chinese was launched with sex tapes of the hospital staff Dr Michael Reilly and his Chinese doctors at a Shanghai department, which were sent to senior executives of the company. The company's investigation on the sex tapes led to the imprisonment of the paid doctor Mark Humphrey and his wife Yu Yingzeng in China, a country which has an opaque judicial system with a 99% crime rate.
