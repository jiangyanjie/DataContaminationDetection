In October 2014, Canadian Prime Minister Stephen Harper announced that he would set forth a plan to send forces to join the coalition for military operations against ISIL , including a squadron of 17 combat aircraft. On 7 October 2014, the House of Commons approved sending 13 Canadian fighter aircraft to join coalition airstrikes against ISIL in Iraq along with 69 special forces troops to train the Iraqi government's military. The Canadian Armed Forces contribution to the military coalition against ISIL was later dubbed Operation Impact. On 30 March 2015, the House of Commons approved a Plan C decision to extend the mission to targets in Syria.
