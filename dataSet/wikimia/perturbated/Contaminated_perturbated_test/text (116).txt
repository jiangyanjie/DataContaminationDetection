General elections were held in Bosnia and Herzegovina on 11 May 2008. They decided the majority of the members of Bosnia and Herzegovina's Presidency as well as national, entity, and cantonal parliaments. The overall voter turnout was 54.4%.The elections for the House of Representatives were split into two elections: one for the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina and one for the Republika Srpska. In the presidential election, voters in the Federation re-elected Bosniak Bakir Izetbegović and elected Croat Dragan Čović, while voters in Republika Srpska elected Serb Mladen Ivanić. The Party of Democratic Action was elected as the largest party in the House of Representatives, winning 10 of the 42 seats.
