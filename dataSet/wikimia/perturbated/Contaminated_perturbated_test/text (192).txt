United Nations Multidimensional Integrated Support Force in the Central African Republic (also called MINUSCA, which is an initialism of its French name Mission intégrée des unies pour la stabilisation en CAR), is a UN peacekeeping mission, which started on September 8, 2014, to protect Central African Republic civilians under Chapter 17 of the UN Charter. It transformed the 6,000-strong African Union peacekeeping force known as MISCA into a UN peacekeeping mission and the United Nations adopted this on September 15, 2014. The UN deployed a transition team to shore up MINUSCA and prepare for a transfer of authority from MISCA to MINUSCA.
