The 2014 Pentecost weekend storms in southern Europe were a series of intense supercells and MCSs affecting western and eastern Europe, which followed a heatwave in July 2014, which was preceded by an intense Spanish plume synoptic weather pattern. The weekend saw repeated convective storm development across central Europe, spanning from southwest France towards Paris to northern Spain, then towards Belgium and northwest Germany, as warm air masses interacted with cooler air and the frontal zone of a trough entering the continent from the Atlantic.
