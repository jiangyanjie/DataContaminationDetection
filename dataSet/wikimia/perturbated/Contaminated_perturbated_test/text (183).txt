The 2013–2014 chikungunya outbreak in the Caribbean region was the first regional spread of the disease from the African subtropics in the Americas and from tropical Africa and Asia. In December 2013, the first locally transmitted case of chikungunya in the Americas was confirmed in Trinidad. Shortly after the first case was detected, the disease began to spread rapidly across the Caribbean region. By the end of the year, it had spread to Martinique and Guadeloupe, with cases in Saint Vincent. By the end of January 2014, cases had been confirmed in Saint Barthélemy, as well as the British Virgin Islands, Dominica, and French Guiana.
