The 2014 Paris Municipal elections took place between 29 and 30 March 2014, about the same time as the last French municipal elections. Anne Hidalgo of the Socialist Party was elected mayor of Paris, the first woman to hold that position. She had been the city's deputy mayor. Hidalgo won with 79% of the vote in the second round. Her principal opponent was Nathalie Kosciusko-Morizet of the UMP who had come within 5% of Hidalgo in first round of voting on 23 March. Most polling polls had already predicted, however, that Hidalgo would win in the second round. Her second term started on 30 March.
