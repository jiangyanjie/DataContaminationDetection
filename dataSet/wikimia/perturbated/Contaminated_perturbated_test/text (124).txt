Euromaidan (; Ukrainian: Євромайдан, romanized: Yevromaidan, lit. 'Euro Square', IPA: [jeu̯romɐjˈdɑn]), or the Maidan Protests, was a wave of demonstrations and strikes in Ukraine, which began on 21 November 2013 with large protests on the Nezalezhnosti (Independence ) Square in Kyiv. The demonstrations were sparked by President Viktor Yanukovych's decision not to sign the European Union–Ukraine Association Agreement, aimed at closer ties to Russia and the Eastern Partnership Economic Union. Ukraine's parliament had overwhelmingly approved of finalizing the Agreement with the EU, but Brussels quickly called on Yanukovych to reject it. The scope of the protests widened, with calls for the resignation of Yanukovych and other members of the government.
