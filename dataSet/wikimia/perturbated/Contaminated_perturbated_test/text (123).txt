Operation Car Wash, also known as Operação Car Wash, was a criminal investigation by the Federal Police of Brazil's federal government. It started in September 2014 and was headed by investigative judge Sérgio Moro, and in December 2016 by Investigative Judge Luiz Antônio Bonat. It resulted in more than a thousand warrants of various descriptions issued to accused . Under the Operation Car Wash , investigations implicate administrative members of the state-owned oil company Petrobras, politicians from Brazil's largest parties (including presidents of the Republic), presidents of the Chamber of Deputies and the Federal Senate, state governors, and businessmen from large Brazilian companies. The Federal Police considers the Operation Car Wash one of the most important in the country's history.
