The World Council of Chemistry performed its 31st Award Ceremony on November 29, 2008 at the Dipoli Hall, Otakaari 1 Building, University of Espoo, Finland. Aalto University acted as the host and co-organizer of the event, which was an opening act prior to the second annual World Academic Summit on the Impact of Chemistry on Science, Technology, Education and Economic Development. The WCC also presented awards to two outstanding professors. Canadian enzymologist Sir Philip Cohen received the Albert Einstein World Award of Science for his forty-year research into cellular communication.
