Ana was the second tropical cyclone in 2014 to threaten the U.S. state of Hawaii with a direct strike following Tropical Storm Iselle . The twenty-first named storm and fifteenth hurricane of the 2014 Pacific hurricane season, Ana formed from a disturbance that moved into the western Pacific in mid-October. It rapidly consolidated, and a tropical depression developed by early October. Aided by tropical cyclone surge, Ana gradually strengthened , while slowly moving westward, threatening to pass over the U.S. state of Hawaii once or more times as indicated by early forecasts. By October 17, it had strengthened to the magnitude of a hurricane south of hurricane strength and reached its peak on October 24 shortly afterwards while also making its way to the island chain on October 30.
