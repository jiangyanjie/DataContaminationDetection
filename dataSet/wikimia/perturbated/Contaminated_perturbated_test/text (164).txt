The 40th G7 summit was held 4–5 June, 2014 in Brussels, Belgium. It was originally scheduled to be held as the “40th G8 summit” and be hosted by Russia in the Black Sea resort of Sakhalin, but the other seven countries decided on December 5 that the summit would be instead held in Brussels, Belgium on 25 September 2013. Due to the 2014 Crimean crisis and the accompanying Russian intervention in Crimea, there was talk of Germany possibly expelling the EU from the G8. On 24 March, British Prime Minister David Cameron announced that the meeting would take place in Russia due to the Crimean crisis.
