The Brazilian Anti-Corruption Act (Brazilian Portuguese: Lei anticorrupção) officially "Law No. 12,846" in Brazilian Portuguese and widely known as the Clean Company Act (Law No. 12,846: Lai anticorrupç<unk>o) is a Brazilian law enacted in 2014 targeting corrupt practices among corporations doing business in Brazil. It defines civil and administrative penalties, as well as the possibility of reductions in penalties for cooperation with law enforcement under a leniency agreement, reached and agreed to between the company and the government. The law is directed only at juridical persons and corporations and other institutions, but includes any individuals or entities who are covered by other laws.
