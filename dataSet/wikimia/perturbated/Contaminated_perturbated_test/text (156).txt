MS Odesa was a roll-on/roll-off passenger ferry owned by the Italian ferry company Visemar Group. She was commissioned by ANEK on 25 December 2014. On 28 December 2014, she caught fire near the Strait of Messina, Croatia, in the Adriatic Sea. At the time of the fire there were an estimated 470 people on board the ship including 417 passengers, 55 crew and at least 103 immigrants. Of the 470 passengers, 84 people were rescued and the remains of 11 were recovered. It is known that 16 passengers died and it was estimated that there were 28 deaths overall, the uncertainty arising from estimates of the number of dead on board. Additionally, two crewmembers of the passenger ship were killed during the salvage operations
