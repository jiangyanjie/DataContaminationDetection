The March against the Cabinet of Oresharski was the name for a series of demonstrations that were held in Bulgaria, mainly in the capital Sofia, against the left-wing coalition cabinet of Oresharski (coalition between Bulgarian Socialist Party and Bulgarian Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS), supported by the ultra-nationalist party DANS). The demonstrations started in May 2013, but actual details did not emerge until early June. While the trigger factor for the demonstrations was the controversial appointment of Alexander Peevski as head of DANS in June 2013, the demonstrations emerged from a variety of causes, to a large extent connected to the constitutional limitations of the BSP-MRF governing coalition and perceived legitimacy issues surrounding political processes .
