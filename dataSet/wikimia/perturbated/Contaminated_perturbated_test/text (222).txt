General elections were held in Bahrain in November 2014 to elect the forty members of the Council of Representatives. The first round of voting took place on 22 November and a second round on 3 December. Almost all of the 34 constituencies were contested in both rounds, and no candidate received a majority.The elections were boycotted by the main Shiite opposition Al-Wefaq. Of the 266 candidates, 22 were women. According to official results, 349,713 Bahrainis, including 729,954 women and 5,039 men, were listed to vote. Although the government announced the voter turnout as 52.6%, the opposition stated that it was only 30%.Independents won 37 of the 41 seats with Sunni Islamists losing two of their seats.
