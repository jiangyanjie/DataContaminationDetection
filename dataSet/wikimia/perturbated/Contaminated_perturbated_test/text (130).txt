The 2014 Australian federal budget was the federal budget to fund government services and operations for the 2014/15 financial year. The budget was the first delivered by the Abbott government, which had been elected following the Opposition Coalition's victory in the 2010 Australian federal election. Treasurer Malcolm Turnbull presented the budget to the House of Representatives on 13 May 2014.The budget featured several measures to address a perceived deficit crisis. This included a proposed dramatic downsizing of government services and proposed major changes to welfare, infrastructure, funding for a medical profession, and spending on military personnel.
