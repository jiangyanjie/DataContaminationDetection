Presidential elections were held in Colombia on 25 May 2014. Since no candidate received 50% of the vote in the first round, a run-off between the two candidates with the most votes was held three weeks later on 15 June 2014. According to the official figures released by the National Registry office (Registraduría Nacional), on 22 May when the electoral cut-off date to reach the first round had been set, 349,872 Colombians were registered and entitled to vote in the 2014 presidential election, while 235,826 Colombians were disenrolled in the process. Incumbent president Juan Manuel Santos was allowed to run for a second consecutive term.
