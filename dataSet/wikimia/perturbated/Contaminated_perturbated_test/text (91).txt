It caused major loss of lives, social suffering, and socioeconomic disruption in the region, mainly in Liberia and Guinea. The first cases were recorded in Guinea in December 2013; later, the disease spread to Liberia and Sierra Leone, with secondary outbreaks occurring in Liberia and Mali. Secondary cases were reported at two medical facilities in the United States and one in Spain. In addition, isolated cases were recorded in Senegal, the United Kingdom and Italy. The number of cases peaked in 2014 and then began to decline gradually, following the commitment of international resources.
