The War of 1812 Monument: A Triumph Through Diversity (Triomphe à la diversité, in French), is a granite and stone memorial, located at Parliament Hill in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada, Canada's capital. Six figures—a First Nations fighter, a soldier from the Canadian Expeditionary Force, a soldier from the 12th Regiment of Fencible Infantry, a Quebec soldier of the Canadian Voltigeurs being bandaged , a female figure, a Royal Canadian Navy mate, and a farmer—represent those who took part on the Canadian side of the War of 1812. The monument is situated across from the National War Memorial with one of the figures on the Monument pointing in its direction.
