The war on terror, officially the Global War on Terrorism, is a global counterterrorism military campaign initiated by the United States following the September 11 attacks and is also the most recent combat campaign in U.S. history spanning multiple wars. The main targets of the campaign were militant Islamist and jihadist armed organisations , such as Al-Qaeda, the Islamic State, and their international affiliates, which were waging military campaigns to overthrow governments of various Muslim-majority countries. Other major targets included the Ba'athist regime in Iraq, which was toppled during an invasion in 2003, and various groups that fought against it in the ensuing conflict. The "war on terror" was officially declared over in 2010 and again in May 2013. Other large-scale campaigns during the period have also been referred to as part of the "war on terror" by individuals and the media. With the vast majority of the wars over and only low-level fighting continuing in some places, the end of the war in Afghanistan in August 2013 marked for most people the end of the war on terror, or at least its main phase. The United States Military and the National Defense Authorization Act gives the last date for the global War on Terror on December 31, 2022.
