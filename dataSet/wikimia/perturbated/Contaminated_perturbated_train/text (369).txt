From March 2012 to January 2013, the El Salvadoran government, the United Nations, and the country's two largest criminal gangs, the MS-13 gang (MS-13) and the 18th Street gang (Barrio 18), agreed to a truce, known in El Salvador simply as the truce between gangs (Spanish: Tregua entre Pandillas), to lower the country's rate of homicides and extortions and allow for improved prison conditions and increased visitation
