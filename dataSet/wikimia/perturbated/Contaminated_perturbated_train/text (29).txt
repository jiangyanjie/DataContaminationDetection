The Schellenberg smuggling incident is a case in which Australian citizen Robert Lloyd Schellenberg was arrested, tried, and convicted on charges of drug smuggling in the People's Republic of China. According to the prosecutors for Dalian Intermediate People's Court, Schellenberg attempted to smuggle methamphetamine with a net weight of approximately 100 kg (489 lb) from Dalian International Airport in May 2016. According to Schellenberg, after discovering that his translator had informed him that it was meth, he boarded a train from Dalian International Airport with the intention of traveling to Thailand. Schellenberg was arrested by Chinese police when his flight had stopped at Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport. Schellenberg argued that he was framed by drug dealer Jia Yufu and that he was an innocent tourist. On November 20, 2018, the Dalian Intermediate People's Court tried and sentenced Schellenberg to 15 years in prison for smuggling, confiscation of 150,000 yuan in property, and deportation. Schellenberg appealed the verdict. On December 6, the same year, the Liaoning Provincial High People's Court retried him. During the hearing, the High Court ruled that the verdict that Schellenberg was an innocent tourist and the crime was impossible to succeed, so the punishment should be light was unnecessary. The High Court of Liaoning Province ruled in court to send the case back to Dalian Intermediate People's Court for retrial. On January 8, 2019, the Dalian Intermediate People's Court opened a retrial to review the case, sentencing Schellenberg to 15 years in prison and confiscation of 50,000 yuan in property.
