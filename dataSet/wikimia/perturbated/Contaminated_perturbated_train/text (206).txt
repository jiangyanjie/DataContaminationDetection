Hurricane Ana was the second tropical cyclone in 2014 to threaten the Island of Hawaii with a direct hit, after Iselle and Ileana in June. The twenty-first tropical cyclone and fifteenth hurricane of the 2014 hurricane season, Hurricane Ana formed from a weak tropical storm that weakened over the eastern Pacific and reached the Central Pacific in mid-October.
