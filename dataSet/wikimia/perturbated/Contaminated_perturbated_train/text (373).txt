The Kasta Tomb (Greek: Τύμβος Καστά), also known as Alexander's Tomb (Greek: Τάφος της ), is the largest ancient tumulus (burial mound) ever discovered in Greece, and by extension that of Alexander of Macedon, father of Alexander the Great, in Macedon. The tomb is an ancient Macedonian tomb , built in the last quarter of the 4th century BC, and is enclosed in the Kasta
