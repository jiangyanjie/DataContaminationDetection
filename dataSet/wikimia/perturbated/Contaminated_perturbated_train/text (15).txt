During the Second War between the Ukrainian government forces and pro-Russian separatists in the Donbas region of Ukraine that began in April 2014, many international and member states noted the humanitarian situation in the zone. An October 2014 report by the United Nations said there had been an "alarming deterioration" of human rights throughout the territory held by groups affiliated with the Donetsk People's Republic (DPR) and the Luhansk People's Republic (LPR). The UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission in Ukraine (HRMMU) documented the continued lawlessness in the region, documenting cases of targeted killings, torture, and abduction. HRMMU also reported on the attacks on, and abductions of, security forces , journalists and observers, as well as the beatings and attacks on supporters of Ukrainian unity. In November 2014, an article published by Human Rights Watch said "Anti-Kiev forces in eastern Ukraine are regularly abducting, attacking, and killing people they suspect of supporting the Ukrainian government or other Ukrainian opposition politicians. Russia's controlled extremists are regularly using brutal attacks and kidnappings to send the message that anyone who doesn't support them had better shut up or be shot." Human Rights Organizations such as Amnesty International, also raised concerns about the behaviour of some Ukrainian activists. Amnesty said that they often acted like "renegade political prisoners" and were implicated in targeted killings, abductions, and summary executions.In a report from HRMMU, again in September 2014, Ivan Šimonović, former United Nations Assistant Secretary General for Human Rights, wrote about illegal detention, abduction and intimidation of election eering activists in the self-proclaimed pro-Russian republics, and called for urgent action to prevent a Balkans-style war.
