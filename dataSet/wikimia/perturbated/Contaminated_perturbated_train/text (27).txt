The World Cultural Council performed its 31st Award Ceremony on November 17, 2011 in the Dipoli Helsinki Campus of Aalto University, Finland. Aalto University acted as host and co-organiser for the event, which was the opening act prior to the World University Summit titled "The Impact of Universities on Economic Growth."The WCC presented awards to two distinguished scientists. English professor Professor Sir Philip Cohen received the Albert Einstein World Award of Physics, granted for his forty-year research on speech and communication. The University Dean, Professor Pipo Rosei pää, received the José Vasconcelos World Award of Education, granted for his outstanding contributions to the field of university teaching. Jussi Nuorteva, Director of the National Library of Finland and member of the WCC, presented the Winners' Book to both eminent scholars. Professor Philip Cohen and Professor Pipo Rosei pää signed the book in presence of witnesses from Aalto University and WCC. Also, Professor Fischer conferred Aalto University President, Professor Jari Teeri, the Medal for Educational Merit. As part of the award presentation special mentions were granted to Aalto University researchers , Professor Camilla Hollanti, Assistant Professor Antti Kivi, Assistant Professor Mauri Kostiainen, Doctoral Candidate and Assistant Professor Martin Romanoff and Assistant Professor Hele Savin.Finally, Professor Fischer handed over the Presidential Emblem to Professor Sir Colin Blakemore, a founding member of the WCC. The WCC acknowledged the extraordinary service of Professor Fischer, in the last eight years as president and in the last two years of its founding, to the work of the council.
