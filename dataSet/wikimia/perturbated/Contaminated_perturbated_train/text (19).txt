The 2014 federal budget was the fourth federal budget to fund government services and operations for the 2014-15 financial year. The 2014 budget was the first budget delivered by the Abbott government, since its victory in the 2011 Australian federal election. Treasurer Joe Hockey presented the budget to the House of Representatives and Senate on 13 May 2014.The budget featured significant changes to government spending to address a perceived deficit , included a proposed dramatic reduction in government bureaucracy, and contained proposed cuts to welfare, education and health, increases to the medical research budget and increased spending on roads. A budget surplus exceeding 1% of GDP was the first budget of any federal government from 1988 until 2015. The budget faced widespread criticism and was widely rejected by the Australian public as reflected in all opinion polls after its release. Opposition to the proposed "unfair" budget measures was backed by Labor in both the upper house and cross-bench, pensioners, the trade union movement, students and welfare, community and disability groups with some taking to the streets in protest.The 2014 budget contained significant policy changes which were contrary to pre-election commitments and promises made by the Liberals in 2008. Critics argue that every one of the following pre-election commitments of Tony Abbott were broken in the first budget: "No cuts to education, no cuts to health, no change to welfare , no change to the GST and no cuts to the ABC or SBS." Echos of the "guns are buried" Fightback! policy package from 1993 are still prominent, with proposals to defer unemployment benefits for six years for under 30s and the removal of tax-free school fees.
