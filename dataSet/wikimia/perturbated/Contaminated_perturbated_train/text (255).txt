The Abkhazian Revolution took place on 5 February 2014, when President Aleksandr Ankvab resigned after hundreds of demonstrators stormed his presidential palace and staged mass demonstrations in the capital Sukhumi and the occupation of his office. On 1 January 2014, Ankvab fled to his hometown of Gudauta and ultimately resigned on 1 February 2014, dismissing the demonstration as an attempted coup. The uprising was attributed to public anger
