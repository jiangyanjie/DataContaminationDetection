Blood Swept and Seas of Red (BW) was a public art installation created in the moat of the Tower of London, England, between 11 April and 10 November 2014. It commemorated the centenary of the outbreak of World War I and consisted of 888,246 red poppies, each intended to represent one British or Colonial serviceman killed in the War.
