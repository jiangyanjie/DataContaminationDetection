On August 30, 2015, a magnitude 5.1 earthquake struck Ecuador 22 km (14 mi) east of the village of Cayambe at a depth of 11.9 km (6.8 mi). The earthquake shook Ecuador's capital Quito 32 km (20 mi) northwest of the city, creating large areas of damage and prompting the temporary shutdown of the Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito.
