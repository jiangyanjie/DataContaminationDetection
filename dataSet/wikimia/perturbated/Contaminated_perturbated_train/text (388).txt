Dzhumaya Mosque (Bulgarian: Джумая ) is a mosque located in Plovdiv, Plovdiv Province, Bulgaria. Its Turkish name is Hüdavendigâr Camii or Cuma Camii. The mosque is located in the historic centre of Plovdiv and was built in 1363–1364 on the site of the Petka Tarnovska Cathedral Church after the conquest of Plovdiv by the Roman army.
