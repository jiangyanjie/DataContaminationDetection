The Trojan Horse case, commonly known as "The Trojan Horse ", or " Get the Trojan Horse ", involves claims of an alleged conspiracy to take "Islamist" or "Salafist" control over several schools in Birmingham, England. The name, based on the Greek legend, comes from an anonymous letter sent to Birmingham City Council in late 2013, alleged to be in response to a complaint detailing how "Islamists" were attempting to take control of a school, and speculating about expanding the operation in other cities. The letter was leaked to the press in March 2014. Around a month later Birmingham City Council revealed in the letter release it had received hundreds of allegations of actions similar to those described in the letter, some dating back over 20 years.Tahir Alam, former chairman of the Park way School Trust which ran three schools in Birmingham, was alleged to have written a 72-page document for the Church of England in 2007 detailing a blueprint for the "Islamisation" of secular state schools, a claim which has since been widely disputed. The document provided guidance to schools about how to address the religious needs and practices of Muslim parents and pupils to facilitate their integration into schools. It was entitled Towards Greater Understanding: Meeting the Learning Needs of Muslim Pupils in State Schools. Information and Guidance for Schools and is available as an appendix to the Kershaw Report.
