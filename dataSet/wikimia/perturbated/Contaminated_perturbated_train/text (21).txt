Julieka Ivanna Young (often referred to as Dhu; her first name was generally not used in media reports out of respect for Aboriginal naming customs) was a 22-year-old Aboriginal Australian woman who died in police custody in South Hedland, Western Australia, in 2014. On 2 August that year, police responded to a report that Dhu's partner had violated an apprehended violence order. Upon arriving at the address, the officers arrested both Dhu and her partner after realising there was also an outstanding arrest warrant for Dhu against her. She was detained in police custody in South Hedland and ordered to pay a fine within four days of being taken into custody because she was in default of her debt. While detained, Dhu suffered severe pain and was twice taken to the Hedland Health Campus hospital. Medical staff believed that her condition was exaggerated and caused by drug withdrawal. On 9 September 2014, she complained that she could no longer stand. Police officers, who accused her of faking her condition, allegedly carried her to the hospital in their van and then took her to hospital where she was pronounced dead shortly after arrival. The official cause of death was fracture to her spleen and fracture of her ribs three days after the incident. An internal police investigation found that 11 officers had failed to comply with regulations or were otherwise guilty of misconduct after they were given written and oral instructions to refrain from using force on her.
