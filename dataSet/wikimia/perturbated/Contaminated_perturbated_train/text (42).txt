The Eurovision Song Contest 2014 is the 59th edition of the Eurovision Song Contest. It took place in Copenhagen, Denmark, following the country's victory at the 2013 contest with the song "Only Teardrops" by Anders de Forest. Organised by the European Broadcasting Union (EBU) and host broadcaster Danish Broadcasting Corporation (DR), the contest took place at Copenhagen 's Central Station and consisted of three live shows on 6 and 8 May, with the final on 9 May. Only Cyprus and Serbia announced that they would not take part. The winner was Austria with the song "Rise Like a Phoenix", performed by Conchita Wurster and written by Tom Mason, Joey Patulka, Ali Zuckowski, and Julian Maas. It was the first time the country had won the contest, after the longest period between victories for any country at the time - 48 years, not having won since 1966. The Netherlands, Sweden, Armenia , Belgium and Turkey rounded off the top five places, with the Netherlands achieving their best result since their victory in 1975, Hungary achieving their best result since their fourth place in 1994, and the UK achieving their best result since 2012.
