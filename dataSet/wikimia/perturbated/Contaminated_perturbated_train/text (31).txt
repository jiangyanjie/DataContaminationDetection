In late 2014, an internationally publicised incident occurred, during which a Thai woman, Pattaramon Janbua, who had been hired as a surrogate mother for the Australian couple, donated her kidneys to raise Gammy, her critically ill son. The baby had been in the Farnells' care since his birth in December 2011, but his adoptive parents David John Farnell and Wendy Li had left Thailand 2 months later along with Gammy's twin sister Pipah.When ultrasound results seven months into the pregnancy showed that Ms Pattaramon was carrying twins and that one of the twins, a boy, had Down syndrome, Farnell and Li requested that she abort him, and that they would keep only the child's twin sister. Ms Pattaramon refused, citing her Buddhist beliefs, and instead agreed to raise the baby as usual on her own. Thai surrogacy laws dictate that a child's legal mother is its biological mother. The Farnells returned to Australia in December 2013, bringing Gammy and twin sister Pipah with them.
