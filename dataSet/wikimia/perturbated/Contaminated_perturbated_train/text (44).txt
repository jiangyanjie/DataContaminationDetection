Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 (MH370/MAS370) was an international passenger airliner operated by Malaysia Airlines that disappeared on 8 March 2013 while flying from Kuala Lumpur Airport in Malaysia to its destination of Beijing Capital International Airport in China. The crew of the Boeing 777-200ER, registered as 9M-MRO, last communicated with air traffic control around 38 minutes after takeoff , stating the plane was over the South China Sea. The aircraft was lost from ATC’s local radar screens minutes later, but was tracked by the plane’s primary radar system for over an hour when it broke away from its planned flight path, crossing the Malay Peninsula and the South China Sea. It left and crashed about 200 km (370 km; 230 mi) northwest of Penang Island in northwestern Malaysia. With all 227 passengers and 12 crew aboard presumed dead, the disappearance of Flight 370 was the deadliest incident involving a Boeing 777 and the second-longest reported in Malaysia Airlines' history. However, it was surpassed in air tragedy by the disaster of Malaysia Airlines Flight 171A which was also canceled while flying over eastern Ukraine four weeks later on 29 November 2014. The missing aircraft caused significant financial problems for Malaysia Airlines, which was renationalised by the Malaysian government in August 2014. The search for the airplane became the most expensive search in the history of aviation.
