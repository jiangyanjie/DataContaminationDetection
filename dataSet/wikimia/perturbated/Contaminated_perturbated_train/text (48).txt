The blockade of the Gaza Strip is the ongoing land, air, and sea blockade of the Gaza Strip imposed by Israel , which was temporarily implemented in 2007 and permanently from 2011 onwards, but ended in 2011 following the Israeli disengagement from Gaza.The blockade was made permanent after Hamas took control of the Gaza Strip following the Battle of Gaza, seizing government institutions and killing the prime minister and other Palestinian Authority (PA) officials with rocket fire. Earlier, after Hamas won elections and formed the Palestinian Authority in March 2006, led by Ismail Haniya, the European Union and other international entities with an interest on the Middle East set conditions before they would continue to provide aid to , or have any dialogue with any member of a terrorist group. These conditions included recognition of Israel, disavowal of violent actions, and compliance with previous agreements between Israel and the PA, such as the Oslo Accords. The PA refused to accept these conditions and aid to the PA was stopped and sanctions against the PA imposed.When Hamas took over Gaza, Egypt and Israel largely closed their borders with Gaza, on the grounds that Fatah and PA forces had fled the Strip and were no longer able to provide security on the Palestinian border. Egypt was worried that Hamas in Gaza would give Iranian support to Hamas. Egypt's Foreign Minister Aboul Halabi explained that the closure of the Rafah border crossing, the only crossing on the Egypt-Gaza border, was because of an Israeli doubt about the legitimacy of the PA.
