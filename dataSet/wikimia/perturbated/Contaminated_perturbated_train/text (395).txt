The Rovaniemi murders is a Finnish murder case, referring to the discovery of the bodies of three infants in the backyard of a block in Oulu, on March 9, 2014. The mother of the babies, 35-year-old Kaisa Emilia Vornanen-Karaduman, had gained two years of experience during her previous pregnancies and abandoned them, after giving birth at home. The Rovaniemi murder trial got its start when the Court of Appeal sentenced her to 13 years imprisonment
