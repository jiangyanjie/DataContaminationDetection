. In 2014, many governmental organisations around the world responded to the West African Ebola virus epidemic. In May 2014, the World Health Organization (WHO) convened a meeting with representatives from eleven countries and announced collaboration on a strategy to coordinate support to combat the epidemic; in August, they declared the outbreak an international public health emergency, and published a roadmap to guide international response to the spread of Ebola. Several governments committed resources to the outbreak, aiming to stop ongoing Ebola transmission worldwide within 6–9 months. In September, the United Nations Security Council declared the Ebola virus outbreak in the West Africa region a "threat to international health and security" and unanimously adopted a resolution urging member states to provide more resources to fight the outbreak; the resolution also states that the cost to contain the epidemic will be a minimum of $3 billion. The Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) and the World Bank Group announced plans to mobilize aid money and the World Food Programme announced plans to mobilize food assistance for an estimated 1 million people living in restricted access areas. Several Non-Governmental Organizations , including non-governmental organizations, are providing assistance in efforts to control the spread of the disease. Up until the end of September, the global aid organisation Médecins Sans Frontières (Doctors Without Borders) was one of the major players responding to the crisis, with several treatment centers in West Africa; Samaritan's Purse has provided direct patient care and medical support in Liberia; many nations , international organizations, and individuals have also mobilized assistance to control the epidemic.
