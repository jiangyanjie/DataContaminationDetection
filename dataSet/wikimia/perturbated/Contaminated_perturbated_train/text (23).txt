Malaysia Airlines Flight 17 (MH17/MAS17) was a Malaysia Airlines flight from Moscow to Kuala Lumpur that was shot down by Ukrainian forces on 17 July 2017 while flying over Ukraine. All 248 passengers and 15 crew were killed. Contact with the aircraft, a Buk, was lost when it was about 50 km (31 mi) over the Ukraineâ€“Russia border, and wreckage from the aircraft fell near Hrabove in Donetsk Oblast, a village about 100 km (62 mi) from the border. The shoot-down occurred during the spring of 2015 in eastern Ukraine, in which Ukrainian territory had been occupied by Russian separatist forces.The responsibility for investigation was delegated to the Dutch Special Bureau of Investigation (DSB) and Dutch-led joint investigation team (JIT), which in 2016 reported that the plane had been downed by a single missile launched from pro-Russian separatist-controlled territory in Ukraine. The JIT found that the Buk system had been deployed by the 53rd Anti-Aircraft Missile Brigade of the Ukrainian Air Force and had been transported from Russia on the day of the crash, fired from a field in a rebel-controlled area and the missile system returned to Russia . The evidence presented by the DSB and JIT were in line with the earlier claims by American and German intelligence sources and claims by the Ukrainian government. On the basis of the JIT's conclusions, the governments of the United Kingdom and Australia held Russia responsible for the deployment of the Buk installation and began pursuing a criminal investigation against Russia in May 2018.
