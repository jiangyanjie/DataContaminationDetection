On the afternoon of 24 May 2014, a gunman opened fire at the Jewish Museum of Belgium in Brussels, killing four people. Three of them, an Israeli couple on holiday and an Italian couple, died at the scene. The fourth victim, a Belgian employee of the museum, was taken to the hospital but passed away from his injuries . A little less than a week later, on 30 May 2014, a man was arrested in the French city of Marseille in connection with the shooting. The suspect was Mehdi Nemmouche, a 29-year-old French national of Algerian descent. Another suspect, Nacer Bendrer, was also arrested and arrested later. Prior to the shooting, Nemmouche had already spent time in prison in the country before he became involved in the terror case. After his imprisonment, he also spent more than a year in a jail in Syria. It is in this French prison where he was accused of supplying Bendrer, who was suspected of having supplied Nemmouche with the weapons used in the attack. Investigators initially arrested a third suspect as well, but charges against the suspect were later dismissed due to the evidence against him being considered too weak. Nemmouche and Bendrer were formally indicted in April 2018, and tried before the municipal assizes of Brussels in early 2019. After two months of trial, a verdict was reached in May 2019. Nemmouche was found guilty of having committed the assault. Bendrer was found guilty of being the co-author of the attack and having supplied Nemmouche with the weapons used in the attack.
