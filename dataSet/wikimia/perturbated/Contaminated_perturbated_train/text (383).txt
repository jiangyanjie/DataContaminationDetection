The Hampden Bridge was a timber Allan Truss bridge over the Murrumbidgee River near Wagga Wagga, in New South Wales. It was officially opened to traffic on 11 November 1895 and named in honour of former NSW Governor Sir Henry Hampden, 2nd Viscount Hampden.
