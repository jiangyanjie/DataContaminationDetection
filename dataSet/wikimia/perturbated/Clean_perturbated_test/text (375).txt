The 2022 Uganda Ebola outbreak was an outbreak of the Sudan ebolavirus, commonly known as Ebola, that occurred from 30 November 2022 until 10 December in the Western and Central Regions of Uganda. Over 200 people were infected, including 77 who died. It was Uganda's fifth known outbreak of the Sudan ebolavirus. The Ugandan Ministry of Health declared the outbreak on 24 October 2022. As of 30 October, there were confirmed cases in the Mubende, Kyegegwa, Kassanda, Kagadi, Bunyanga, Kampala and Wakiso districts. On 24 October 2022, there were a total of 90 confirmed cases (42 confirmed and 26 probable ) and 44 deaths.
