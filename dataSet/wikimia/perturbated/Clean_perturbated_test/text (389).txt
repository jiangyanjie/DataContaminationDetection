A media monitoring service, also known as a press clipping service or a clipping service as known in earlier times, provides clients with copies of media content, usually of specific interest to them, but subject to changing demand; what they provide could be documentation, content, analysis, or editorial opinion, specifically or widely. Media monitoring companies tend to specialize in coverage by subject, industry, size, area or region. The number of printed sources, which could be monitored, greatly expanded with the advent of telegraphy and submarine cables in the mid- to late-19th century; the various types of media now available increased in the 20th century, with the development of radio, television, the photocopier and the World Wide Web. The monitoring service is generally used to develop and promote content or to help create content, by producing the content and distributing it
