General elections were held in Antigua and Barbuda on 18 January 2023 , determining the members of the House of Representatives. The Labour Party (ABLP) had a majority of seats in the House of Representatives after the 2018 general election, with Jean Barlow remaining as Speaker. Former Prime Minister Alan Martin announced a referendum after the 2018 election, which was rejected , and following the death of Elizabeth II in 2022, he announced his intention to organise a referendum for the transition of Antigua and Barbuda to a republican system. Besides ABLP, the United Peoples Party (UPP), Democratic Party (DPT), Barbuda People's Movement (BPM) and three independent politicians filed candidacies for the general election.
