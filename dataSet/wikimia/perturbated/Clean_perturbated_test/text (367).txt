Fit for 55 is a package by the European Union designed to reduce the European Union's greenhouse gas emissions by 55% by 2030. The package was unveiled in July 2021 by the European Commission. Following an accelerated legislative process, the plans may become law in 2022. Measures include support for high-density renewables, and a tariff called the Carbon Border Tax, which would reduce the tax on emissions for the EU from countries lacking sufficient greenhouse gas reduction measures of their own. It also proposes to expand the European Union Emissions Trading System to include heat. In addition to the net-zero scenario from the International Energy Agency, the package contains more measures to ensure clean energy remains competitive.
