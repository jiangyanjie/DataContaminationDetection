On 4 January , Israeli Justice Minister Yariv Levin announced his intention to introduce a number of changes to the judicial system and the balance of powers within it. On 11 January, Levin published details of his proposed changes, which include changes to the judiciary, executive and legislative processes and functions. The government's decision to pursue these changes has sparked intense controversy . Opposition leaders, senior past civil servants, economists, political activists and others have harshly criticized the proposed changes, arguing they will undermine democracy and effectively grant the government unchecked power. The opponents of the justice reform include reservists in Israel's security forces, who responded by threatening that they will stop conducting reserve duty service if the law is progressed through as legislation.
