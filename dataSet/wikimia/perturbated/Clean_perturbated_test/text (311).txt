The hour travel time is the average time spent by a person for travel per day. Its value is one hour, or half an hour for a one-way trip. The hour travel time was named after Italian physicist Cesare Marchetti, who himself attributed the "one hour" finding to transportation analyst , Yacov Zahavi.Marchetti posits that although the context of urban planning and transport may change, and certain people live in towns, others in cities, people gradually adjust their lives and conditions (e.g. the position of their homes relative to their workplace) such that the average travel time stays approximately constant. Ever since these observations, people have kept the average time spent per day for travel the same, even though the distance traveled could change due to changes in the means
