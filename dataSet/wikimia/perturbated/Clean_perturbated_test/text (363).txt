A news bureau is an office for gathering or distributing news. Similar terms are used for various kinds of news bureaus, often to describe a geographic location or area of coverage: ‘Tokyo bureau’ refers to a given news operation's office in Tokyo; 'foreign bureau' is a generic term for a news office set up in a country other than the primary operations center; a ‘Washington bureau’ is an office located in the capital that covers news , current events, or other topics in the United States. The person in charge of a news bureau is usually called a news bureau chief. The term bureau chief comes from the word bureau desk, which generally describes the editorial function of assigning work to other staff, and otherwise coordinating, the distribution of news.
