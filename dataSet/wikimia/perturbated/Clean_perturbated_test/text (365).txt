2018. Parliamentary elections were held in Estonia on 5 March 2019 to elect all 101 members of the parliamentary assembly. The results, officially published in March, indicate the victory of the Reform Party, which won 37 seats in total, while the People's Party of Estonia (EKRE) came second with 17 seats. The Centre Party won 16 seats, a loss of 10, while Estonia 200 won two seats, only gaining representation in Riigikogu. After the parliamentary election in 2019, the Centre Party, led by Lauri Ratas, formed a government with Ratas serving as prime minister. His government was brought down in January 2021 after a corruption investigation, and Kaja Kallas led by the Reform Party formed a coalition with the Centre Party but their government collapsed in June 2022.
