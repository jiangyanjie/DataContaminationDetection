Two motions of opposition in the minority government of Prime Minister Élisabeth Borne will be debated on April 13, 2023 in the National Assembly. One motion was proposed by a cross-party grouping that included the Socialist Workers Party and the regionalist LIOT (written by Bertrand Laliberté and defended by Charles de Courson); the other by the right-wing populist National Front (written by Marine Le Pen and defended by Laure Lavalette). The two motions are in response to the government's use of constitutional article 49.3 to pass a controversial law that raised the retirement age from 62 to 64.
