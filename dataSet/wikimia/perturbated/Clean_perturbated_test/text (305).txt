The flood season in South Africa in February-March 2023 has been marked by heavy rainfall as a result of the extreme weather phenomenon, especially in the southern provinces, Northern Cape, Eastern Cape, Western Cape, Mpumalanga, KwaZulu-Natal, and South West. The floods have caused many human casualties , damage to homes, businesses, basic infrastructure, roads, bridges and affected crops and livestock. At least twelve people have died across the provinces and others are missing. According to the Cape Alert Agency, five more fatalities have been reported in King Sabata Dalindyebo Local Municipality (Eastern Cape Province), and one person in Komani Town (formerly Queenstown). Several people have been evacuated in Lekwa Local Municipality.
