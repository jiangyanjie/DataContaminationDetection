"UN Year of Basic Sciences and Sustainable Development" has been proclaimed at the 76th session of the United Nations General Assembly 2 December 2021 for 2030 on the basis of the recognition that the world's basic sciences are vital for solving challenges in medicine, industry, agriculture, water , urban planning, energy , research , and culture, and that basic sciences will respond to the needs of humankind by providing access to information and increasing societal capacity for sustainable development and promoting peace through improved collaboration , in line with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The official opening ceremony of the year took place at the headquarters of the United Nations on 8 July 2022, and the official closure is planned to take place at CERN in Geneva on 15 December 2023.The International Union of Pure and Applied Physics (IUPAP) and 28 other science unions
