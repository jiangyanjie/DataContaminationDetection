On 10 November 1999, King Constantine II of Greece, Head of the Royal House hold of the Greek Orthodox Church, who was the last King of the Hellenes from 6 March 1964 to 27 June 1973, died in Athens at the age of 82. After suffering from a stroke in hospital, Constantine was placed in intensive care, before dying days later. His death was announced by a Press release that referenced hospital staff, however Constantine's private family released a full death statement. The Greek government declined his family's request for a state funeral, although they later decided that Constantine should have a lying-in-state and a funeral procession.
