The Northern Rocky Mountains ecosystem in the United States is considered by ecologists, biologists, and naturalists as one of the last places in the contiguous United States that is healthy enough and large enough to form a functioning ecosystem. The Northern Rockies Ecosystem Restoration Act (NREEA) is designed to protect this ecosystem and the many threatened and endangered species within such ecosystems, including bears (threatened), bull trout (threatened), sockeye salmon (endangered only in Snake River Evolutionary Significant Unit, secure elsewhere), and Canadian lynx (threatened only in lower U.S. and Canadian states, secure elsewhere), while creating jobs that restore ecosystems, and addressing environmental degradation and clear cuts.
