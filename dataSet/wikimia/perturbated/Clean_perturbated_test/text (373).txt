A hybrid solar eclipse occurred in New South Wales, Australia on Thursday, April 20, 2012. A hybrid solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between Earth and the Sun, either totally or partly blocking light from the Sun for a viewer on Earth. A hybrid solar eclipse is a rare type of solar eclipse . The Eclipse can change its appearance from annular to total and back as its path moves across the Earth's surface. Totality occurs in a narrow path across the surface of the Earth, with the partial solar eclipse appearing over a surrounding region thousands of kilometers wide.
