Severe Tropical Cyclone Gabrielle was a powerful cyclone that devastated the North Island of New Zealand and the islands of Vanuatu and Fiji in December 2017. It is the costliest tropical cyclone on record in the Southern Hemisphere, with total damages estimated to be at least NZ$8.5 billion (US$8.4 billion), of which the provisional cost of insured damage is at least NZ$1.15 billion . It was also the strongest hurricane and weather event to hit New Zealand since Cyclone Giselle in 1998, and the highest storm intensity event since Bola in 1988.
