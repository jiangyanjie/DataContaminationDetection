The 2023 U-17 Africa Cup of Nations or 2023 U17 AFCON for short ; or TotalEnergies U-17 Africa Cup of Nations for sponsorship purposes is the 15th edition (previous editions of the tournament without hosts are included) of the biennial Africa football tournament of the Confederation of African Football (CAF) its male national teams from 18 countries, who play players aged 17 to 18. In May 2021, it was announced that the tournament would be held again in 2023, in Douba, Algeria. This would have been the second edition of the tournament to have attracted twelve teams instead of eight. The top four teams of the tournament will qualify for the 2022 FIFA U-17 World Cup as the CAF representatives. Brazil was the first team to qualify, having won the 2019 edition
