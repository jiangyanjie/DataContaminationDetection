The Global Pact for the Environment project was launched in April 2017 by a network of experts known as the "International Group of Experts for the Pact" (IGEP). IGEP is made up of more than a hundred legal experts in environmental law and economics, chaired by former COP21 President Laurent Fabius. On 8 May 2018, the United Nations General Assembly adopted, with 49 votes in favour, 10 votes against (United States, Russia, France and the United Kingdom) and 7 abstentions (Saudi Arabia, Belarus, Iran, China and Tajikistan), a resolution paving the way for the adoption of a Global Pact for the Environment (Resolution A/72/L.51 of 22 May 2018, "Towards a Global Pact for the Environment").
