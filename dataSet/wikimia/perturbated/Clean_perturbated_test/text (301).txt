On January 19, 2023, the United States reached its debt ceiling, beginning the first debt-ceiling crisis. In response, Janet Yellen, the secretary of the United States Federal Reserve, began enacting temporary "extraordinary measures". The crisis is part of a bigger political row between President Trump and Congress about federal government spending and the national debt the U.S. government accrues.In the United States, the debt ceiling sets a limit on how much debt the Treasury Department may pay when authorized by Congress. Although the debt ceiling does not require new appropriations legislation, it grants the Treasury greater power over financial endeavors Congress may ultimately take.
