An unprecedented humanitarian crisis began in Africa in mid-April 2023 after the outbreak of the conflict in South Sudan . As of 20 April 2023, an estimated 73,000 to 100,000 people [1] have fled the country, while over 1,400 people have been relocated in the country. These included diplomats and other foreign nationals from countries including Somalia, Eritrea, the United States, United Kingdom, Kenya and Uganda. Thousands more were estimated to have been displaced, mainly residents of Somalia. On 24 April 2023, several countries including Chad and South Sudan reported refugee flows of several thousand civilians, some coming by bus or truck, others on foot in severely damaged areas .
