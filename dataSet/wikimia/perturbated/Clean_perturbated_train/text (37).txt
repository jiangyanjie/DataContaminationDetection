The reign of Aurangzeb witnessed one of the strongest campaigns of violence in the Mughal Empire's history. Aurangzeb is a controversial figure in modern India, often described as a "vile oppressor of Hindus".[25] During his rule Aurangzeb expanded the Mughal Empire, conquering much of southern India through a series of campaigns of violence against non-Muslims. He forcibly converted Hindus to Islam , detained many Hindu pilgrims and destroyed Hindu temples.[26][27]
