The 65th annual Grammy Awards ceremony was a music awards ceremony held at the Crypto.com Arena in Los Angeles, California on February 5, 2023. It recognized the best recordings, compositions, and artists of the year – October 1, 2021 to September 30, 2022 – as determined by the members of National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences. The recipients were announced on November 15, 2022. South African comedian Trevor Noah, who hosted the 63rd and 64th Grammy Awards, returned again.Beyoncé received the most nominations (nine) and wins, followed by Radio City Music Awards nominees (four), including Kendrick Lamar with eight nominations and three wins, and Adele and Brandi Carlile with four nominations each. With a total of 88 nominations, Beyoncé tied Drake and husband Jay-Z as the most nominated artists in Grammy history. Bugs Bunny's Un o (2022) became the first Spanish-language album to be nominated for Album of the Year.With her win in the Best Dance/Electronic Album category, Beyoncé passed fellow Canadian Georg Solti for the record of most Grammy awards in the last five decades with 32. Harry Styles won Album of the Year for Harry's House, becoming the first British solo artist to win since George Michael in 1989. Lizzo won Record of the Year for "Take You Back in Time", becoming the first black woman to win the award since Whitney Houston in 1994.
