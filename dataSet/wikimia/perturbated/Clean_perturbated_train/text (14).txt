The Reddings was an American funk, soul and disco band, founded by Otis Redding's sons Dexter (bass and lead vocals) and Otis Redding III (March 28, 1963 – April 18, 2023 age 53), together with Paul Redding (drums, keyboards, and lead vocal).Their most well-known songs include "Remote Control" (1980) / "Call the Law" / "Funkin on the One" / "What I Love (Is What It 's About)" / "From the Dock of the Bay" / "Rockin' in Love" (1983) and "The Awakening" (1980), a bass-drums-duet which is often performed by Les Claypool (Primus) and on his album Highball with the Reddings. Their third studio album The Reddings was released on May 25, 1988, and reached a peak of #88 on the US Billboard 200 album chart. It featured a hit single "Call The Law," which reached a new career high position at #2 on the Billboard Black Singles Chart on May 26, 1988. In December 1980, "Remote Control," recorded on the first album by the Reddings (The Awakening), climbed to #6 on the Billboard Hot Soul Singles chart, and peaked at #30 on the Billboard Hot 100. The tune was sampled in episodes of the 1980s television programme, the City with Cincinnati. Written by Nick Mann, Bill Beard, and Bobby Fortune , Washington, D.C. based members of Last Mile, "Remote Control" continues to receive airplay in England, Belgium, France, Italy, and the Netherlands.
