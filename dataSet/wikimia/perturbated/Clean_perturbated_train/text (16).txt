This was the first ceremony to be held in person , as both ceremonies in 2021 and 2022 were held virtually. Additionally, it was the first ceremony to be held in Japan, as previous physical ceremonies were held in the United States. Anime that aired on television or online in Japan between November 2021 to September 2023 was eligible for nominations for the awards. The ceremony was hosted by voice actress Sally Shigetani and presenter Jon Kabira, with voice actor Kenichiro Yoshida opening the ceremony. Previous winners ALI and AKLO, among others, portrayed by Sei Kohta Yamamoto performed at the event while singer Kaki of Nogizaka46, comedian Kendo Kobayashi, entertainer Roland, and producer Hiroki Suzuki presented the awards and announced winners together with various special guests, including actors, directors, and entertainers. Sony Music Solutions collaborated with Crunchyroll in planning and operations, with Telescope working on the voting process. Crunchyroll streamed the ceremony live on Facebook, Twitch, as well as via SonyLIV in India. Voting for the Anime of the Year category included "social voting" for the first time, which allowed the participants to vote for the category through social media using the necessary hashtags or "tags". The list of nominees was announced on the first day of the ceremony, January 19. Voting closed on April 15. The organization announced the judges and categories on March 6, 2022.
