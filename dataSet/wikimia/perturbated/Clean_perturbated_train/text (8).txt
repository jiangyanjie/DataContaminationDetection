The International Year of Basic Sciences for Sustainable Development was proclaimed by the 76th session of the United Nations General Assembly in December 2021 for 2022, stressing that the applications of basic sciences are vital for advances in industry, agriculture, water resources, energy planning, environment, communications and culture, and that basic sciences rupture technologies respond to the needs of humankind by contributing to human progress, increasing quality of life and promoting peace and collaboration toward Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The official opening ceremony of the Year is planned to be held in UNESCO in Paris on 8 July 2022. The closing ceremony is planned to be held at CERN in Geneva on 15 July 2022. The International Union of Pure and Applied Physics (IUPAP) and 28 other science unions and organizations constituted a Steering Committee, chaired by Michel Spiro , and including interim chairs Jean Trần Thanh Vân, Michael Martin, and Karuna Shastri, which from January 2019 to December 2017 was dedicated to the process leading to the proclamation by the United Nations General Assembly of 2022 as the International Year for Basic Science for Sustainable Development. Over 80 other organizations, among them many national science academies and their networks, are supporting the initiative.UNESCO acts as the official organizing agency and focal point of the Year. The programme for the Year is developed in collaboration with other relevant entities of the United Nations, the IUPAP, CERN, and their associated organizations and federations from around the world.
