Its Secretary, Ministry of Law and Justice & other connected cases , among an ongoing collection of landmark cases of the Supreme Court of India, which are set to decide whether to extend the right to marry and establish a family to queer Indians. A 5-judge Constitution Bench, consisting of Chief Justice of India D.Y. Chandrachud, Justice S.K. Kaul, Justice Ravindra Jaiswal, Justice S.D. Sharma and Justice T. Narasimha, will hear 20 petitions , and 52 petitioners.The petitioners, both queer couples and individuals, request recognition of the right to marry and establish a family based on protections of the fundamental human rights, including equality, dignity, personal privacy and personal autonomy, and freedom of religious expression. Delhi Commission for Protection of Human Rights, a statutory body of Aam Aadmi Party-led Delhi Government, intervened to support extending the right to marry and adopt a child to queer Indians.The respondent, the Union Government led by the Bharatiya Janata Party leadership and its statutory body National Commission for Protection of Child Rights, opposes extending the right to marry and establish a family for queer Indians due to societal, cultural and governmental differences, consistent legislative policy, popular morality and majoritarian views. Islamic organisations Jamiat Ulema-e-Hind and Jain Shia Party have intervened to oppose the right to marry and establish a family for queer Indians on cultural and societal differences.
