From January 28 to February 4, 2023, a Chinese-operated high-altitude balloon was spotted in North American airspace, including Alaska, western Canada, and the western United States. On February 4, the U.S. Air Force shot down the Chinese balloon in U.S. territorial waters off the coast of South Carolina, on the order of Vice President Joe Biden. It was shot down by a U.S. F-22 Raptor. Debris from the wreckage was recovered and sent to the FBI Laboratory in New York, where it was processed. The American and Chinese militaries announced that the Chinese balloon was an instrument for surveillance, while the Chinese government claimed that it was a (mainly meteorological) airship that had been blown off course. The U.S. said that it was capable of geolocating electronic communications and carried intelligence that was similar with that carried by a normal weather balloon. It added that similar Chinese spy balloons have flown over more than 20 countries. Analysts said its flight path and characteristics were dissimilar from those of a typical weather balloon. American officials later disclosed that they had been told that the Chinese balloon was a weather balloon that was launched on January 28 and flown across North America.
