The Houston Film Critics Society Awards were announced on February 18, 2023, at the Midtown Arts & Cultural Houston (MATCH) in Texas. The nominees for Best Independent Film were announced on November 5, 2023, with The Banshees of Inisherin and Everything Everywhere All at Once leading the nominees with nine each. Both films won the most awards with three , with Everything Everywhere All at Once winning Best Picture. The nominees for the Texas Independent Film Award were announced on December 7, 2022. Alongside special guests, local film critics and industry notables, the event featured live, abridged performances of the five Best Original Song nominees, which went to "Naatu Naatu" from "My Way," along with the results of the Texas Independent Film Award competition. "As the industry continues to define how we experience the magic of film, moviemakers continue to imagine creative stories about America and the world," said Douglas Harris, HFCS President. "This year, the films we chose showcase a community daring to rethink, innovate and reinvent," she concluded.The performances of the nominated songs were performed by the voices of Sheleah DeMarco, Ryan Starnes, and Sankar a from "My Way" under the direction of director Scott Benton, and the presence of the Consuls from Argentina, Belgium, Germany and Korea enhanced the presentation of Best Foreign Language Feature. Following the two-hour production, guests adjourned to the Gallery at MATCH for a reception featuring Everything Everywhere All at Once-themed bagel sandwiches from the cafe's chef, who also happens to be the owner of Ziggy Gruber 's " Ziggy's New York Delicatessen" and Barry Shapiro of "Bagel Express".
