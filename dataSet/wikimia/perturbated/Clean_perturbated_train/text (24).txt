The 60th British Academy Film Awards, more commonly known as the BAFTAs, were held on 19 February 2023, honouring the best national cinema films , at the Royal Festival Hall in London's West End. Presented by the British Academy of Film and Television Arts, accolades were handed out for the best feature-length film and television programmes of any kind produced in any country, which were screened at British cinemas in the year to November 2018. Awards winners were announced on 19 January 2023 via an international and global livestream, hosted by Hayley Atwell and Toheeb Jimoh, from the arts charity's newly redeveloped HQ at 195 Piccadilly, London. The EE Rising Star Award nominees, which is the category voted for by the British public, were announced on 17 January 2023.The German-language epic anti-war film Quiet on the Border received the most nominations with fourteen, tying the record set by Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon (2000) as the most nominated German language film in Academy history; winning seven of these, including Best Film, Best Director (Edward Berger) and Best Cinematography. As the first nomination Not in the English Language, the World War I epic set a new record for the most nominations for a non-English language film. The ceremony was co-hosted by producer and Academy Award-nominated actor Hugh E. Grant and British Academy film producer Alison Hammond, who also hosted backstage interviews. Presenters Vick Hope and BBC Radio 1 film producer Stephen Fry hosted the red carpet pre-show.
