Severe Tropical Cyclone Gabrielle was a severe tropical cyclone that devastated the North Island of New Zealand and affected parts of Vanuatu and the Solomon Islands from 12 to 23 February 2023. It is the costliest natural disaster on record in the Southern Hemisphere, with total damages estimated to be at least NZ$13.5 billion (US$2.5 billion), of which the provisional cost of insured damages is estimated at at least NZ$7 billion (US$730 million). Gabrielle was also the deadliest cyclone and weather event to hit New Zealand since Cyclone Giselle in 1994, and Cyclone Bola in 2001. The fifth named cyclone of the 2022–23 Australian region cyclone season, and the first named cyclone of the 2022–23 South Pacific cyclone season, Gabrielle was first noted as a developing tropical low on 10 February 2022 where it was located to the south of the Pacific Ocean before being classified as a tropical cyclone called Gabrielle by the Bureau of Meteorology. The system was classified as a Category 3 severe tropical cyclone before moving into the Pacific basin, then rapidly degenerated into a subtropical low on 11 February 2023. Norfolk Island was placed under a red alert as the cyclone approached, and rain and wind warnings were issued for parts of the North Island of New Zealand.
