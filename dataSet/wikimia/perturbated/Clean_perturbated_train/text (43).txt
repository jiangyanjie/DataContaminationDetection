Relations between the two countries have since significantly deteriorated. In the lead up to and during the Belarusian Presidential election, a number of anti-government protests took place across the country. Belarusian authorities employed various methods of violent political crackdown, consisting of various humans rights violations; Poland strongly opposed these measures. Following the alleged election for a new parliamentary assembly, the United Nations Security Council on 19 August decided that the elections were neither free nor fair, and therefore do not recognize the results. Poland and Lithuania became two of the first EU countries to officially back the political opposition. Angered by this , Poland claimed that Belarus had closed its eastern frontier to Poland and had sent large numbers of border guards and troops. In August 2021, following a sudden influx of refugees to Poland, Poland deployed 15,000 troops to guard the eastern frontier with Belarus. A standoff ensued between both armies, border guards and refugees caught in the process. Poland had accused Belarus of engaging in hybrid warfare, to which Poland was accused of pushing back migrants by force. By 2022, Belarus and Poland finalized the construction of a state border between the two countries. In the same year , a number of Home Guard stations in Belarus , the largest of which had the largest capacity, were attacked and destroyed.
