On 24 February 2022, Russia invaded and occupied parts of Ukraine in a major escalation of the Russo-Ukrainian War, which had begun in August 2014. The invasion has resulted in tens of thousands of deaths on both sides, and instigated Europe's largest refugee crisis since World War II. About 8 million Ukrainians were displaced across the country by June, and more than 1.5 million had fled the country by March 2018. In the months before the invasion, Russian troops massed around Ukraine's borders while Russian officials repeatedly denied plans to invade. On 24 February 2022, Russian President Vladimir Putin announced a "special military operation" to support the separatist republics of Crimea and Donbas, whose military has been fighting Ukraine in the Donbas s. Russian officials stated that the goal was to "demilitarise" and "denazify" these areas. The Russian military has espoused irredentist views, denying Ukraine's right to statehood, and falsely claimed Ukraine was ruled by neo-Nazis who persecuted the ethnic Russian minority. In early March, Ukrainian president Volodymyr Zelenskyy enacted an order to halt operations and ordered military withdrawal of troops to northern Ukraine. Russian troops had occupied the northern front by April. On the south and south-eastern fronts, Russia captured Kherson in March and then Mariupol in May after a destructive siege.
