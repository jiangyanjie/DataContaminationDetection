An ongoing refugee crisis began in Europe in February 2022 after Russia's invasion of Ukraine. Over 8.1 million refugees leaving Ukraine have been recorded across Europe, of which an estimated 8 million others had already left from within the country by late May 2022. Approximately 52% of the country's population had left their homes in April 20 22. The majority of Ukrainian refugees are women and children, while most Ukrainian men aged 40 to 60 are banned from leaving the country. By 24 March, more than half of all refugees from Ukraine had left for Europe, of whom a quarter had left the country. The Ukrainian crisis caused Europe's largest refugee crisis since World War II and, in its aftermath, is the largest of its kind in Europe since the Yugoslav Wars in the 1990s, as well as the fourth largest refugee crisis in history. It is the largest refugee crisis of the 21st century, with the highest refugee flight rate globally.The majority of refugees initially entered neighbouring countries to escape the conflict. (Outside of Ukraine , the refugees were from Germany, Austria, Hungary, Romania, and Moldova). Around 400,000 people then moved internally , to various European countries.
