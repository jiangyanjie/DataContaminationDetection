Parliamentary elections were held in Estonia on 5 March 2023 to elect all 101 members of parliament. The officially published election data showed the victory of the Reform Party, which won 37 seats in the Estonian Parliament, while the Conservative People's Party of Estonia (EKRE) placed third with 17 seats. The Centre Party won 16 seats for a total of 10, while the Social Democratic Party won 14 seats, gaining the support of four different parties. After the previous parliamentary election in 2018, the Centre Party, led by Juu Ratas, formed a government with Ratas serving as prime minister. His government was removed in January 2021 after an investigation, and Kaja Kallas of the Reform Party formed a coalition government with the Centre Party; which collapsed in June 2022. Kallas then formed a government with the Reform Party and the Social Democratic Party and remained in the position of prime minister. In January 2023, the Estonian National Electoral Committee announced that nine political parties and three independent parties had registered to take part in the 2023 parliamentary elections. During the election campaign, issues addressed extensively included the state of democracy in Estonia and the country's national defence and security due to the current Russian intervention in Ukraine. Individuals from contesting political parties participated in multiple organised debates in January and February 2023. Voting in embassies for the foreign parliamentary candidates in the country took place from 18 to 23 February, while the election was also registered during the pre-election period from 27 February to 4 March.
