2023 Conference on Climate Change, more commonly referred to as 2023 Geneva Conference, was an international conference organized to provide aid to Pakistani Government after the devastating flood in January 2018. The conference was co-hosted by the Pakistani Government and the United Nations, held on 9 January 2023 in Geneva, Switzerland. Shehbaz Sharif, the Prime Minister of Pakistan, and Antonio Guterres, the Secretary-General of the United Nations, jointly hosted the one-day conference, the more than 450 attendees including private donors, international financial institutions, and government representatives from around 40 countries.In the conference, Pakistan introduced the “Resilient, Recovery and Reconstruction Framework” (4RF) and seek s support and long-term partnerships for its implementation. The 4RF document covers the mechanism and institutional arrangements for providing aid in an open, transparent, inclusive, and collaborative manner, and it also contains a strategy and sequenced plan that is set at the federal and provincial level.The government also created a Post-Disaster Needs Assessment (PDNA) with the support of the United Nations system, World Bank Group, Asian Development Bank, and European Development Bank. The PDNA places the flood damage at over US$14.9 billion, the economic damage at over US$15.2 billion, and the cost of reconstruction at over US$16.3 billion.Pakistan raised more money than it had asked at this conference, more than $9 billion, from international financial institutions, donor agencies and development partners for the relief and reconstruction of flood-affected areas.
