Wrestle Kingdom 17 is a two-day professional wrestling pay-per-view (PPV) event co-produced by the New Japan Pro Wrestling Network and Japan Pro Wrestling Noah . The first day of the event took place on January 4, 2023, at the Tokyo Dome Show in Tokyo, Japan, and the second day of the event took place on January 21, 2023, at the Yoyogi Sports Arena in Yoyogi, Japan. It was the 32nd January 4 th Show and the 17th promoted under the same name. The event was held in honor of the NJPW founder Antonio Inoki, who died on October 1, 2018. In the main event of night 1, Kazuchika Okada defeated Jay Lee to win the IWGP Japan Superstar Championship. In other prominent matches, Kenny Omega defeated Will Power to win the IWGP United States Heavyweight Championship, Keiji Muto teamed with Hiroshi Tanahashi and Daisuke Umino to defeat Los Ingobernables de Japon (Tetsuya Naito, Sanada and Bushi) in Muto's final NJPW match, and Kohei Kairi defeated Tam Nakano to retain the IWGP Women's Championship – following the match, Kairi, who was formerly known as Missy in WWE, made her NJPW debut. The event's first night also notably featured appearances from wrestlers signed to All Elite Wrestling, WWE, NJPW's sister promotion World Wonder Ring Stardom, and other promotions. In the main event of the second night, which was co-promoted by Noah, Tetsuya Naito defeated Kazuhiko Sanada – after the match, Naito was announced as Keiji Muto's last ever opponent at the NJPW Grand Final s.
