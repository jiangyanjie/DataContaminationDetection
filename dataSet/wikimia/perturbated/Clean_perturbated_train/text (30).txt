The Good News International Ministries, more commonly known as the Malindi Church, and previously as the Servant P. N. Mackenzie Ministries, is a faith-based political and religious movement based in Shakahola, Kilifi, Kenya, that was founded by Paul Nthenge Mackenzie and his wife in 2003. Though often listed as a Christian organisation, many of its teachings are opposed to Christianity. The group attracted international attention in April 2023 when it is claimed that Mackenzie and his wife instructed members to starve themselves en masse to "meet God" which resulted in the deaths of at least 110 people. The group, often described as a cult, is adamantly anti-Western, with amenities such as health care and sports being dismissed as "evils of western life" and with leaders of the government, the United Nations, and the Catholic Church as "tools of the devil". The group also devotes much of its teachings to the end of the world. In 2015, an GNIM member claimed to be an apologist for the End-Time Message of William Branham. Homicide detectives working the case said the group was radicalized by its teachings, leading to their deaths.Mackenzie founded the GNIM in 2003 and accumulated a sizable following, largely due to his claim to his followers that he had personally met directly with God. Beginning in the early 2016s, Mackenzie and his wife began to receive a wave of criticism for the internal workings of the organization. In 2017, Mackenzie and his wife faced several charges relating to the church.
