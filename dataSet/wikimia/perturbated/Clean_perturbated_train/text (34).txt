Despite having a multi-layered and religiously tolerant Constitution that aims to increase religious representation and participation in all levels of society including the government, the active role played by autonomous bodies such as National Human Rights Commission of India, National Commission for Minorities, and the great work being done by civil society organisations, sporadic and sometimes serious acts of religious violence tend to occur as the root causes of religious violence often run deep in history, culture and the social structure of India.[4][5][6][7]
