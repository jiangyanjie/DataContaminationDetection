Protests at several locations in Ireland started in early November 2022 after the development of sites in various parts of the country as temporary refugee shelters by the Department of Children, Equality, Disability, Integration and Youth (DCEDIY), as it attempts to accommodate the influx of 65,000 refugees during 2022. Protests have been held in East Wall, Ballymun, Drimnagh and elsewhere in Dublin, Fermoy and Mallow in County Cork, Kill, County Kildare, Lismore, County Waterford, and Mullingar. The protests raised similar concerns over refugee selection discrimination and the lack of provision of prior information to residents.
